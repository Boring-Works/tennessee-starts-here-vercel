**Date:** January 30, 2026

**Context:** Evidence Room contains 38 primary sources (95% publication-ready); strategy focuses on sustainable expansion, quality maintenance, and institutional growth

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Evidence Room launches with 38 documents that meet professional archive standards. This strategy outlines how to grow this collection to 100+ documents over 24 months while maintaining publication quality, expanding narrative coverage, and developing sustainable partnerships with archives, universities, and historical organizations.

**Key Principles:**

- Quality over quantity (never sacrifice verification for volume)
- Continuous improvement (every document released should be as good as the last)
- Inclusive representation (Cherokee, enslaved people, women, local voices)
- Community partnership (leverage existing archives and expertise)
- Sustainable workflows (systems that scale without heroic effort)

## PART 3: HOW TO CONTINUOUSLY ADD DOCUMENTS

### A. The Document Addition Process (Step-by-Step)

#### Step 1: Identification & Research (2-4 hours)

**Goal:** Confirm document exists, verify authenticity, assess difficulty

**Checklist:**

- [ ] Primary source confirmed (letter, document, record)
- [ ] Author & date identified
- [ ] Historical significance documented
- [ ] Narrative gap filled (doesn't duplicate existing document)
- [ ] Source location identified (archive, online database, etc.)
- [ ] Acquisition difficulty assessed (easy/medium/hard)
- [ ] Estimated time to publish: **\_** hours

**Tools:**

- Founders Online (founders.archives.gov) - best starting point
- American State Papers (archive.org) - federal documents
- Library of Congress Collections (loc.gov)
- Archive search guide (see Appendix B)

**Output:** Candidate document record with decision (APPROVE / REJECT / DEFER)

---

#### Step 2: Source Acquisition (1-6 hours depending on difficulty)

**Easy (1 hour):** Already digitized, transcribed, publicly available

- Copy text from Founders Online, American State Papers, or LOC
- Examples: Most Founding-era federal correspondence
- Action: Save PDF/text, create working file

**Medium (2-4 hours):** Digitized but requires permissions, reformatting, or light transcription

- Request from archive, format for publication
- Examples: University archive materials, regional collections
- Action: Contact archive, obtain permission, reformat

**Hard (4-6 hours):** Original manuscript requires microfilm request, transcription from scratch

- Submit digitization request, wait 1-2 weeks
- Transcribe handwritten/faded documents
- Verify against original
- Examples: Private papers, local records
- Action: Negotiate with archive, allocate transcription time

**Output:** Clean text file + source attribution + permissions documentation

---

#### Step 3: Metadata Completion (1 hour)

Use this YAML template (from existing documents):

```yaml
---
id: [document-id-lowercase-with-dashes]
title: [Document Title]
date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
content_type: [letter/report/petition/treaty/journal/journal-entry/proclamation/law]
source: [Primary source collection name + URL]
collection: [collection-id: blount-papers, treaty-documents, etc.]
author: [author-id from people/]
recipient: [recipient-id, if applicable]
people_mentioned:
  - [person-id]
  - [person-id]
responds_to: [document-id if part of conversation]
physical_location: [Where at Rocky Mount this is relevant, if applicable]
verification:
  status: [verified/single-source/disputed-with-disclosure]
  source_count: [number of independent sources confirming document exists]
  method: [How verified - "Compared against original in [archive]", "Cross-referenced with [sources]", etc.]
description: [2-3 sentence summary of content, for search/discovery]
---
```

**Verification Status Explained:**

- **Verified:** Found in 2+ independent authoritative sources (Founders Online + Library of Congress, etc.)
- **Single-Source:** Found in only one trusted archive, but document is authentic (physical original exists)
- **Disputed-With-Disclosure:** Document exists but has questions about authorship, date, or content interpretation. Include full disclosure in historical context.

**Output:** Document metadata complete, ready for editorial review

---

#### Step 4: Content Formatting (1-2 hours)

Structure the document file:

```markdown
---
[YAML metadata from Step 3]
---

[Opening paragraph: Who, what, when, where in brief]

## [Primary Source Content Section]

[Full or partial document text]

<passage id="quote-id">
[Key passage if document is long]
</passage>

---

## Historical Context

[2-3 paragraphs explaining the document's significance]

### Key Details

- **Written from:** [Location]
- **Written to:** [Recipient, if letter]
- **Significance:** [Why this matters to the founding narrative]

### About the Author

[1-2 paragraphs on the person who wrote this]

### About the Recipient

[1-2 paragraphs on the person receiving this, if applicable]

### Broader Context

[How does this fit into the larger 1770-1796 narrative?]

---

## Connections

**Related documents in Evidence Room:**

- [Document Title](./document-id.md)
- [Document Title](./document-id.md)

**Further Reading:**

- [Academic source](URL)
- [Archive source](URL)
```

**Key Rules:**

- Use `<passage id="identifier">` tags for important quotes (makes them quotable/searchable)
- Keep historical context 300-500 words (readable but substantive)
- Always include "About the Author/Recipient" sections
- Cross-reference related documents for discoverability

**Output:** Fully formatted document, ready for QA

---

#### Step 5: Quality Assurance & Editorial Review (1-2 hours)

**Checklist (Patricia Alvarez or designated QA lead):**

- [ ] Metadata is complete and accurate
- [ ] YAML frontmatter is valid (test with markdown parser if possible)
- [ ] Document text matches source (spot-check 3-5 passages)
- [ ] Transcription is clean (no OCR artifacts, readable)
- [ ] Historical context is accurate (fact-check key claims)
- [ ] Connections to other documents are correct
- [ ] Verification status is appropriate
- [ ] Description is compelling and searchable
- [ ] No broken internal links
- [ ] Tone is neutral and professional
- [ ] Grammar and spelling checked

**Decision:** APPROVE / REQUEST CHANGES / REJECT

**If Changes Needed:** Return to content team with specific feedback. Typical revisions take 30 mins - 1 hour.

**Output:** QA sign-off + publication approval

## PART 4: QUALITY CONTROL PROCESSES

### Principle: The 95% Standard

Every document published should be at the same quality level as the current 38. Never compromise on verification, clarity, or scholarly rigor for speed.

### Quality Gates

#### Gate 1: Initial Verification (Before acquisition)

- [ ] Document genuinely exists (not legend/myth)
- [ ] Author and date confirmed
- [ ] Source location identified
- [ ] Acquisition is realistic (time/budget/permissions)
- Decision: Proceed to acquisition or defer

#### Gate 2: Source Acquisition Verification

- [ ] Text matches published source
- [ ] Transcription accurate (spot-checked)
- [ ] No OCR errors or artifacts
- [ ] Permissions obtained if needed
- [ ] Attribution correct

#### Gate 3: Content Verification

- [ ] All claims in historical context fact-checked
- [ ] Cross-referenced against 1-2 academic sources
- [ ] Dates and names accurate
- [ ] Context is balanced (not biased toward one narrative)
- [ ] Connections to other documents verified

#### Gate 4: Editorial QA

- [ ] Metadata complete and accurate
- [ ] Format consistent with other documents
- [ ] No broken links or references
- [ ] Tone professional and neutral
- [ ] Grammar and spelling clean

#### Gate 5: Accessibility Check

- [ ] Document is discoverable (good description, tags)
- [ ] Historical context explains WHY this matters
- [ ] Lay reader can understand significance
- [ ] Links to related documents work
- [ ] Technical issues resolved

## PART 9: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCIES

### Risk 1: Slow Document Acquisition

**Risk:** Partner archives take longer than expected, digitization requests stall
**Mitigation:**

- Maintain "quick-win" queue of 10-15 pre-verified, easy documents
- Have backup sources ready (Founders Online, LOC always available)
- If one partner stalls, switch to different archive

**Contingency:** If acquisition slows, shift focus to deeper research on existing documents (add more historical context, related materials)

---

### Risk 2: Quality Slips Under Pressure

**Risk:** Rush to hit document targets, compromise on verification
**Mitigation:**

- Never publish without Patricia Alvarez QA approval
- Track "time per document" metric; if dropping below 3 hours, pause and audit
- Monthly quality review: are we maintaining >95% verification?

**Contingency:** If quality declining, reduce monthly target from 10 to 6 documents until quality recovers

---

### Risk 3: Decline in User Engagement

**Risk:** After launch excitement, pageviews plateau
**Mitigation:**

- Publish on predictable schedule (1-2 docs/week creates momentum)
- Highlight documents on social media, email newsletter
- Create seasonal themes ("Women's Month," "Military History")
- Solicit user feedback; publish "corrected documents" when users submit improvements

**Contingency:** If engagement drops >30%, assess: Are documents discoverable? Are they compelling? Redesign Evidence Room landing page or create collections/themes to drive engagement.

---

### Risk 4: Partnership Burnout

**Risk:** Archive partners overwhelmed by requests, discontinue collaboration
**Mitigation:**

- Set clear expectations upfront: "We'll request 3-4 documents per quarter"
- Always say "thank you" — share metrics showing impact
- Reciprocate: help partners with grant applications, co-publish findings
- Formalize agreements to protect both sides

**Contingency:** If partner relationship dissolves, have diverse partner portfolio (never relying on one source for >30% of documents)

---

### Risk 5: Resource Constraints

**Risk:** Budget cuts, staff turnover, no funding for contractors
**Mitigation:**

- Document all workflows so replacements can step in quickly
- Use in-kind contributions (volunteer historians, Rocky Mount staff)
- Pursue grant funding early (awards take 6-12 months)
- Design lean workflow that works with 1-2 people if needed

**Contingency:** If budget cuts force staffing reductions, shift from 10 docs/month to 4-6 docs/month. Quality maintained, just slower growth.
