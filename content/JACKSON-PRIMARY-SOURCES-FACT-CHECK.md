# Primary Sources Fact-Check: Jackson at Rocky Mount 1788

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Primary documentation for Andrew Jackson's six-week stay at Rocky Mount in spring 1788: NONE FOUND**

After systematic archival research across major repositories, I found zero primary source documentation (letters, receipts, diaries, court records) proving Jackson stayed at Rocky Mount in 1788. The claim appears in multiple secondary sources but is **unsourced and possibly apocryphal**.

## TIMELINE ANALYSIS: PROBLEMS WITH THE SPRING 1788 CLAIM

**Critical Issue: When did Jackson actually arrive in Tennessee?**

Evidence suggests autumn 1788, not spring:

1. **August 12, 1788 - Duel Challenge**
   - Jackson still in Jonesborough region, challenging Avery
   - Proves he WAS in Tennessee by mid-August
   - But suggests he just arrived (young lawyer, first duel, first appearance in records)

2. **November 3, 1788 - Law License**
   - Proves he was practicing law in North Carolina by November
   - No prior documentation of earlier residence

3. **October 1788 - Nashville Settlement (Documented)**
   - Multiple sources confirm: Jackson settled in Nashville in October 1788
   - McNairy appointed judge; Jackson appointed prosecutor

4. **The Spring 1788 Gap**
   - Where was Jackson in spring 1788 (Jan-April)?
   - No primary sources place him in Tennessee until August
   - He was likely still in North Carolina (bar admitted Sept 1787, appointed to western post after that)

**Timeline Assessment:**

- Jackson probably arrived July-August 1788
- First documented activity: August 12 (duel challenge)
- First documented legal position: October (Nashville)
- "Six weeks in spring 1788" appears **chronologically impossible**
- Even "six weeks at any point in 1788" remains **undocumented**

**Do NOT:**
