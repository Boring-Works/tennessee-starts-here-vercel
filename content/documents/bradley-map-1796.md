## A Map of the State of Tennessee

_Compiled from the surveys in the Surveyors General Office, 1796_

<passage id="map-description">This map, prepared shortly after Tennessee achieved statehood, shows the boundaries of the new state and the principal settlements, rivers, and roads. The Cherokee boundary established by the Treaty of Holston is clearly marked, as are the lands ceded to the United States.</passage>

### Notable Features

<passage id="map-features">The map shows Knoxville as the seat of government, with Rocky Mount marked along the Great Road from Virginia. The Holston, French Broad, and Tennessee rivers are depicted, along with the Great Indian Warpath and other principal routes of travel. County boundaries reflect the territorial organization as of statehood.</passage>

### Cartographic Notes

<passage id="map-context">This map represents the accumulated geographical knowledge of the Southwest Territory period. Surveyors had traversed much of the region in connection with land grants and the establishment of the Cherokee boundary, producing data that enabled this comprehensive representation of the new state.</passage>

---

_This map is significant for showing Tennessee at the moment of statehood, with the Cherokee lands and American settlements clearly delineated._
