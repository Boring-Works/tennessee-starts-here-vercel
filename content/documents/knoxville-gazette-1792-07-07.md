## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 36 - July 7, 1792

---

### Anniversary of the Treaty

<passage id="treaty-anniversary">This week marks the first anniversary of the Treaty of Holston, concluded between Governor Blount and the Cherokee Nation on the second of July, 1791. The year past has been one of general peace, and the benefits of the treaty are evident in the growth and prosperity of this territory.</passage>

### Population Growth

<passage id="population-increase">The population of the territory has increased remarkably since the conclusion of the treaty. New families arrive weekly from Virginia, North Carolina, and other states, and settlements are extending rapidly into the lands acquired from the Cherokee.</passage>

### Independence Day

<passage id="independence-celebration">The anniversary of American Independence was celebrated in Knoxville on the fourth instant with appropriate ceremonies. A salute was fired at sunrise, and the citizens assembled to hear an oration on the blessings of liberty. The day concluded with a public dinner at which patriotic toasts were drunk.</passage>
