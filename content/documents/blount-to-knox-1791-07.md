## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_July 5, 1791_

Sir,

<passage id="treaty-concluded">I have the honor and satisfaction to inform you that on the second instant, a treaty was concluded with the Cherokee Nation at this place. Forty of their chiefs and warriors affixed their marks to the instrument, including the Hanging Maw, the Bloody Fellow, and John Watts.</passage>

The treaty establishes a definite boundary between the Cherokee lands and the territory of the United States. <passage id="boundary-established">The line runs from the point where Clinch River crosses the boundary established by the treaty at Hopewell, thence up the Clinch to the ridge which divides the waters of the Tennessee from those of Cumberland, and along said ridge to a point from which a line shall be drawn to the mouth of Duck River.</passage>

The Cherokee have ceded to the United States a considerable tract of country, in consideration of which they are to receive an annual payment. <passage id="annual-payment">The sum agreed upon is one thousand dollars, to be paid in goods suitable to their circumstances, which I hope will contribute to their comfort and civilization.</passage>

I have the honor to be, with great respect, your most obedient servant,

William Blount
