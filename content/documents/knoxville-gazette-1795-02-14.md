## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 4, Number 7 - February 14, 1795

---

### Statehood Movement

<passage id="statehood-call">The people of this territory, having now attained the population required for statehood, are calling for a convention to form a constitution. Governor Blount has indicated his support for this measure, and it is expected that delegates will be elected in the coming months.</passage>

<passage id="convention-plans">The proposed convention will meet in Knoxville to frame a constitution and form of government for the new state. Upon adoption of this constitution by the people, application will be made to Congress for admission to the Union.</passage>

### Public Sentiment

The sentiment in favor of statehood is nearly universal throughout the territory. The people are eager to enjoy the full rights of American citizens, including representation in the councils of the nation.
