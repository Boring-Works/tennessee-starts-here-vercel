## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_November 20, 1791_

Sir,

<passage id="capital-moved">I have removed my residence from Rocky Mount to the new town of Knoxville. Buildings are being erected, and the town promises to become a place of considerable importance. Its situation on the Holston River affords advantages for trade and communication.</passage>

The Knoxville Gazette, the first newspaper west of the Appalachians in this region, has commenced publication. <passage id="gazette-established">Mr. George Roulstone, the printer, has undertaken this enterprise, and I trust that a press will contribute much to the improvement and information of the people.</passage>

The affairs of the territory continue to prosper. <passage id="territory-prosperity">New settlers arrive constantly, and the population increases rapidly. The lands ceded by the Cherokee are being surveyed and opened for settlement, which will add to the strength and resources of the territory.</passage>

I have the honor to be, with the highest respect, your most obedient servant,

William Blount
