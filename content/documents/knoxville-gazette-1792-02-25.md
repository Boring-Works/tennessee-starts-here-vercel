## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 17 - February 25, 1792

---

### Cherokee Delegation in Philadelphia

<passage id="delegation-news">We are informed by letters from Philadelphia that a delegation of Cherokee chiefs, headed by the Bloody Fellow, has visited the seat of federal government. They were received with attention by the President and other officers of government, and an additional article to the Treaty of Holston was concluded, increasing the annual payment to the Cherokee Nation.</passage>

<passage id="bloody-fellow-honored">The Bloody Fellow, formerly a distinguished warrior, is said to have conducted himself with great dignity during the visit. The President conferred upon him a new name, Iskagua, signifying "Clear Sky," as a token of the peace now established between the two peoples.</passage>

### Local News

The spring season approaches, and the inhabitants are making preparations for planting. The lands opened by the late treaty are said to be of excellent quality for cultivation.
