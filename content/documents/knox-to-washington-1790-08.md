## Letter from Secretary Knox to President Washington

_August 17, 1790_

Sir,

In reply to your inquiry respecting the proper residence for the Governor of the Southwest Territory, I beg leave to offer the following observations:

<passage id="holston-recommended">The settlements on the Holston River appear to be the most suitable location for the seat of government. This region is the most populous and best established of the frontier communities, and its central position will facilitate communication with the Indian nations.</passage>

<passage id="cobb-residence">I am informed that there is a respectable residence available in that quarter, belonging to a Mr. William Cobb, which might serve as temporary accommodations for the Governor until a more permanent establishment can be made. This dwelling is said to be commodious and well suited to the dignity of the office.</passage>

<passage id="cherokee-proximity">The proximity of this location to the Cherokee towns will enable the Governor to maintain close attention to Indian affairs. A treaty with that nation should be among the first objects of his administration, and a convenient location will much facilitate the negotiation.</passage>

I have the honor to be, with the highest respect, your most obedient servant,

H. Knox
