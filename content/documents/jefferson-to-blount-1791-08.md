## Letter from Secretary Jefferson to Governor Blount

_August 31, 1791_

Sir,

I have the honor to inform you that the treaty which you concluded with the Cherokee Nation on the second of July has been received and laid before the Senate.

<passage id="treaty-received">The President is highly satisfied with the conduct of the negotiation. The boundaries established appear reasonable and the terms just to both parties. The annual payment agreed upon is within the limits of your instructions and will be provided for in the appropriations of Congress.</passage>

<passage id="senate-consideration">The treaty will be submitted to the Senate for their advice and consent at the commencement of their next session. I have no doubt that it will receive their approbation, the terms being such as the government can with honor and justice fulfill.</passage>

<passage id="cherokee-relations">You will continue to cultivate friendly relations with the Cherokee chiefs, and to impress upon them the good faith of the United States in performing its engagements. The lasting peace which this treaty promises will be of incalculable benefit to the inhabitants of your territory.</passage>

I have the honor to be, with great respect, your most obedient servant,

Th: Jefferson
