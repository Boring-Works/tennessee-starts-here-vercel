## Commission of William Blount as Governor

_June 8, 1790_

George Washington, President of the United States of America.

<passage id="appointment">To all who shall see these presents, Greeting: Know ye, that reposing special trust and confidence in the integrity, prudence and abilities of William Blount of North Carolina, I have nominated, and by and with the advice and consent of the Senate, do appoint him Governor in and over the territory of the United States of America south of the river Ohio, and Superintendent of Indian Affairs for the Southern District.</passage>

<passage id="powers-granted">He is therefore carefully and diligently to discharge the duties of Governor and Superintendent of Indian Affairs by doing and performing all manner of things thereunto belonging. And I do strictly charge and require all officers and soldiers under his command to be obedient to his orders, and all citizens of the territory to pay due respect to him in his said offices.</passage>

<passage id="commission-duration">This commission to continue in force for the term of three years from the date hereof, unless sooner revoked by the President of the United States.</passage>

Given under my hand and the seal of the United States, at New York, this eighth day of June, in the year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety, and of the Independence of the United States of America the fourteenth.

G. Washington

By the President:
Th: Jefferson, Secretary of State
