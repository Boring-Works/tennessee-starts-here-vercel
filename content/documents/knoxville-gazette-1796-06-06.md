## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 5, Number 23 - June 6, 1796

---

### TENNESSEE ADMITTED TO THE UNION

<passage id="statehood-achieved">We have the supreme satisfaction of announcing that Tennessee has been admitted to the Union as the sixteenth state. The act of admission was passed by Congress on the first instant, and the great object for which the people have so long labored is now accomplished.</passage>

<passage id="celebration">The intelligence was received in Knoxville with demonstrations of the liveliest joy. The bells were rung, cannon fired, and the citizens assembled to celebrate this happy event. In the evening, the town was illuminated, and the people gave themselves up to rejoicing.</passage>

<passage id="blount-senator">William Blount, Esq., who has governed this territory with distinguished ability since its organization, has been elected by the legislature to represent the new state in the Senate of the United States. His long experience and intimate knowledge of our affairs eminently qualify him for this important trust.</passage>

### The Road Traveled

From the establishment of federal government at Rocky Mount in October 1790, through the Treaty of Holston in 1791, to this day of statehood, Tennessee has traveled a remarkable path. The state enters the Union strong, growing, and full of promise.
