## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 5, Number 3 - January 17, 1796

---

### Constitutional Convention

<passage id="convention-assembles">The convention for forming a constitution for the proposed State of Tennessee assembled in this town on the eleventh instant. Fifty-five delegates, elected by the people from every part of the territory, are in attendance. Governor Blount opened the proceedings with an address congratulating the delegates on this important occasion.</passage>

<passage id="constitution-work">The convention is now engaged in the momentous work of framing a constitution. The instrument is to be modeled generally on those of the older states, with such provisions as are suited to our particular circumstances. It is expected that the work will be completed within a few weeks.</passage>

### A New State

Upon the adoption of this constitution, our territory will assume the dignified rank of a sovereign state, entitled to equal representation in the councils of the nation.
