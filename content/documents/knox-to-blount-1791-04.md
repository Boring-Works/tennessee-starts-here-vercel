## Letter from Secretary Knox to Governor Blount

_April 22, 1791_

Sir,

The President having determined that a treaty should be held with the Cherokee Nation, I am directed to communicate to you his instructions respecting the same.

<passage id="treaty-authority">You are hereby authorized and empowered to negotiate and conclude a treaty of peace and friendship with the Cherokee Nation of Indians, on behalf of the United States. The treaty should establish definite boundaries between the Cherokee lands and the territory of the United States, and should provide for the regulation of trade and intercourse between the two peoples.</passage>

<passage id="boundary-instructions">With respect to boundaries, you will endeavor to obtain a cession of as much land as can be procured consistently with the principles of justice and the future peace of the frontier. The settlements already made by citizens of the United States should be confirmed, but care must be taken not to press the Indians beyond what they may be willing to concede.</passage>

<passage id="annuity-authority">You are authorized to promise the Indians an annual payment, not exceeding one thousand dollars in goods, as compensation for lands ceded. This payment should be represented as a proof of the friendship and liberality of the United States.</passage>

I have the honor to be, your obedient servant,

H. Knox
