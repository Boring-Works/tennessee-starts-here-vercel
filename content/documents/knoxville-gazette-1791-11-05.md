## The Knoxville Gazette

### Volume 1, Number 1 - November 5, 1791

_Printed by George Roulstone, at his printing office in Knoxville_

---

<passage id="first-newspaper">The Knoxville Gazette, being the first newspaper published in the territory of the United States south of the Ohio, makes its appearance this day. The printer flatters himself that he shall be able to render his paper useful and entertaining to the public.</passage>

### Foreign Intelligence

_From the latest dispatches received..._

### Domestic News

<passage id="territory-progress">The affairs of this territory continue to prosper. Governor Blount has established the seat of government at this place, and the town of Knoxville grows daily. New settlers arrive constantly from the old states, and the lands recently acquired from the Cherokee Nation are being opened for settlement.</passage>

### Advertisement

<passage id="land-office">Notice is hereby given that the Land Office for the territory will be opened at Knoxville on the first Monday of December next. All persons having claims to lands within the territory are requested to present the same for registration.</passage>

---

_Subscription: Two dollars per annum, payable half-yearly in advance._
