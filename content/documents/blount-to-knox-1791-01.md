## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_January 8, 1791_

Sir,

<passage id="courts-established">I have the satisfaction to inform you that courts of law have been established throughout the territory. The judges appointed have entered upon their duties, and the administration of justice proceeds in an orderly manner.</passage>

The people of this territory are industrious and enterprising. <passage id="economic-development">New settlements are forming, farms are being cleared, and the commerce of the territory increases daily. The navigation of the Tennessee and Holston rivers affords convenient transportation for the produce of the country.</passage>

I continue to receive intelligence from the Cherokee country. <passage id="cherokee-intelligence">The chiefs remain well disposed toward peace, though some of the younger warriors, particularly among the Chickamauga towns, are said to favor continued hostility. I am using every effort to strengthen the influence of the peace chiefs.</passage>

Your most obedient servant,

William Blount
