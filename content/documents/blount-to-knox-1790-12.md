## Letter from Governor Blount to Secretary Knox

_December 15, 1790_

Sir,

<passage id="cherokee-chiefs-visit">Several Cherokee chiefs, including the principal chief known as the Hanging Maw, have visited me at Rocky Mount. They express a desire for peace and appear sincere in their professions of friendship toward the United States.</passage>

I have assured them of the good intentions of the federal government and have promised that a formal treaty will be negotiated in the coming year. <passage id="treaty-promise">They have expressed satisfaction with this assurance and have agreed to restrain their young men from acts of hostility, provided the settlers likewise refrain from encroachments on their lands.</passage>

The situation on the frontier remains delicate. <passage id="frontier-tensions">Some settlers continue to press beyond the established boundaries, which occasions uneasiness among the Indians. I have issued proclamations forbidding such encroachments and shall enforce the laws as strictly as circumstances permit.</passage>

I have the honor to be, your obedient servant,

William Blount
