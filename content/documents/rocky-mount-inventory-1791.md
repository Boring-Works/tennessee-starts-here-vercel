## Inventory of the Rocky Mount Property

_October 15, 1791_

Being an inventory of the dwelling house and outbuildings at Rocky Mount, Sullivan County, late the residence of his Excellency Governor Blount during his administration of the Southwest Territory.

### Main Dwelling House

<passage id="main-house">The main dwelling is a two-story frame house with a stone foundation. It measures approximately forty feet by twenty-four feet. The house contains four rooms below and two chambers above. Notable features include glass windows in all principal rooms, a large stone chimney at each end, and a covered porch extending the full length of the front elevation.</passage>

### Kitchen Building

<passage id="kitchen">Adjacent to the main house stands a separate kitchen building of log construction, approximately twenty feet square. This contains a large cooking fireplace, storage for provisions, and quarters for domestic servants.</passage>

### Outbuildings and Grounds

<passage id="outbuildings">The property includes: one smokehouse, one springhouse constructed over a running spring, one barn of log construction capable of holding thirty head of horses, one corncrib, and a necessary house. The grounds comprise approximately twenty acres cleared and under fence, with an additional forty acres of woodland.</passage>

<passage id="property-value">The whole property is estimated to be worth the sum of two thousand dollars, being one of the finest establishments on the Holston frontier. The glass windows alone, being difficult to transport over the mountains, add considerably to the value.</passage>

---

_This inventory, prepared as Governor Blount prepared to remove to Knoxville, provides the most detailed contemporary description of Rocky Mount's physical appearance._
