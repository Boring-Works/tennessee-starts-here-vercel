## By the President of the United States of America

### A Proclamation

<passage id="treaty-ratified">Whereas a Treaty of Peace and Friendship was made and concluded on the second day of July, in the year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety-one, between the United States of America and the Cherokee Nation of Indians, by William Blount, Governor in and over the territory of the United States south of the river Ohio, and Superintendent of Indian Affairs for the Southern District, on the part of the United States, and the undersigned Chiefs and Warriors of the Cherokee Nation, on the part of said nation:</passage>

<passage id="senate-advice">And whereas the Senate of the United States did, on the eleventh day of October, one thousand seven hundred and ninety-one, advise and consent to the said Treaty in the words following, to wit: "Resolved, two thirds of the Senators present concurring, That the Senate do advise and consent to the ratification of the Treaty of Peace and Friendship, made and concluded on the second day of July, 1791..."</passage>

Now, therefore, be it known that I, George Washington, President of the United States, having seen and considered the said Treaty, do, by and with the advice and consent of the Senate, accept, ratify and confirm the same, and every clause and article thereof.

<passage id="binding-law">In testimony whereof, I have caused the Seal of the United States to be affixed to these presents, and signed the same with my hand. Done at the City of Philadelphia, the eleventh day of November, in the year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety-one, and of the Independence of the United States of America the sixteenth.</passage>

G. Washington

By the President:
Th: Jefferson
