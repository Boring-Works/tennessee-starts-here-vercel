At William Cobb's Washington County...

<passage id="glass-windows">
On the 11th instant, I arrived in this country, and was received with every mark of attention and gladness that I could have wished. I am very well accommodated with a Room with Glass Windows, Fireplace, etc., etc., at this place.
</passage>

## Historical Context

This letter, dated October 20, 1790, was written by William Blount to Henry Knox, the Secretary of War, shortly after Blount's arrival in the Southwest Territory. Blount had been appointed Governor of the Southwest Territory and Superintendent of Indian Affairs by President George Washington.

### Key Details

- **Written from:** Rocky Mount, the home of William Cobb, Washington County
- **Arrival date:** October 11, 1790 (the "11th instant" referenced in the letter)
- **Location:** Near the fork of the Holston and Watauga Rivers (present-day Piney Flats, Tennessee)

### Significance

The mention of "Glass Windows" is notable because glass windows were a status symbol on the frontier in 1790. Most frontier cabins had only wooden shutters or oiled paper for window coverings. The presence of glass windows at Rocky Mount demonstrated the relative sophistication and prosperity of William Cobb's residence, which would serve as the first capital of the Southwest Territory.

### About the Recipient

Henry Knox served as Secretary of War under President Washington from 1789 to 1794. As Superintendent of Indian Affairs, Blount reported to Knox on matters related to relations with Native American nations, particularly the Cherokee.
