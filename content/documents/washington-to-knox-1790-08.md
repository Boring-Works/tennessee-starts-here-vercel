## Letter from President Washington to Secretary Knox

_August 13, 1790_

Dear Sir,

The appointment of Mr. Blount having been confirmed by the Senate, it becomes necessary to give him proper instructions for the conduct of his government.

<passage id="residence-question">Where ought the Governor to reside? Should it be at the seat of the existing settlements on the Holston, or at some point more convenient to all parts of the territory? The location of the capital will have much influence on the future character of the settlements.</passage>

<passage id="indian-relations">The management of Indian affairs will require particular attention. The Cherokee Nation, being the most considerable of the tribes in that quarter, should be cultivated with care. A general treaty, establishing boundaries and providing for the regulation of trade, appears to be necessary.</passage>

<passage id="federal-authority">It is essential that federal authority be firmly established from the commencement of the government. The inhabitants of the frontier have been accustomed to act with little restraint, and they must be made to understand that they are now subject to the laws of the United States.</passage>

I am, dear Sir, your most obedient servant,

G. Washington
