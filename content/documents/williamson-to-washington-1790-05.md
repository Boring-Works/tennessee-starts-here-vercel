## Letter from Hugh Williamson to President Washington

_May 28, 1790_

Sir,

I take the liberty of recommending to your notice William Blount, Esquire, of North Carolina, as a person well qualified for public employment in the western territory.

<passage id="blount-qualifications">Mr. Blount is a gentleman of considerable property and influence. He has been actively engaged in the affairs of the western settlements and understands the character of the people. His connections and his knowledge of land affairs in that quarter give him particular advantages for managing the complex interests of the frontier.</passage>

<passage id="faction-unifier">The western territory is much divided by factions. Mr. Blount, having the confidence of men on different sides of these disputes, may be able to unite them in support of the federal government. I know of no other person who commands such general respect among the inhabitants.</passage>

<passage id="indian-experience">Mr. Blount has had considerable experience in Indian affairs and conducted negotiations with the Cherokee during the late war. This knowledge will be invaluable in establishing peaceful relations with the tribes who border on the new territory.</passage>

I have the honor to be, with the greatest respect, your most obedient servant,

Hugh Williamson
