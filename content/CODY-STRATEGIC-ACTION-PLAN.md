## PART 3: BLOWBACK RISK ASSESSMENT

_Where you could get hurt, and how to avoid it_

### RISK #1: Jackson Claim Controversy

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM-HIGH
**Potential Blowback:**

- Local history community feels you're "debunking" beloved story
- Board members or donors invested in Jackson narrative push back
- Media frames it as "historic site admits Jackson never visited"

**The Facts:**

- Dendrochronology proves current buildings are from 1826-1830
- Zero primary sources for Jackson's 1788 visit
- Tennessee Encyclopedia calls Christopher Taylor house in Jonesborough "stronger documented evidence"
- You cannot credibly claim "verified" status

**Safe Path Forward:**

1. Don't hide the evidence - transparency is your friend
2. Frame as "scholarly honesty" not "debunking legend"
3. Say: "The tradition is long-standing and may have some basis, but the buildings visitors see today were built later, and no primary documentation of Jackson's visit has been found"
4. Create the positive counter-narrative: "Rocky Mount's VERIFIED significance is as the first territorial capital where the Treaty of Holston was negotiated" (this is true and documented)
5. Consider: Brief board discussion before publishing to avoid blindsiding anyone

**DO NOT:**

- Claim the visit is "verified" (you'll be embarrassed when questioned)
- Hide the dendrochronology evidence (it's published scholarship)
- Make Jackson the centerpiece of your Evidence Room (make the Treaty of Holston the star)

---

### RISK #2: Cherokee Nation Consultation Backfire

**Risk Level:** HIGH (if done poorly) / LOW (if done well)
**Potential Blowback:**

- Cherokee scholars critique your representation as superficial or appropriative
- You invite consultation, get criticism, and then don't change anything (worst possible outcome)
- Cherokee Nation publicly distances from project you've claimed as "partnership"

**The Facts:**

- Current Evidence Room has 0% Cherokee-authored documents
- Cherokee Representation report correctly identifies this as "critical gap"
- Partnership Strategy recommends $12K-45K for Cherokee partnership phase
- You likely don't have that budget

**Safe Path Forward:**

1. **Start small and authentic** - Add the FREE Cherokee documents from Founders Online (Hanging Maw letter, Coyatee attack report)
2. **Don't claim "partnership" until you have one** - Say "Cherokee perspectives" not "Cherokee Nation partnership"
3. **If you pursue consultation:** Approach with genuine humility. Ask "How should we tell this story?" not "Please validate what we've already written."
4. **Budget reality:** $2K-5K Phase 1 (add 4-6 Cherokee-authored documents) is achievable. $45K Phase 3 (scholar essay + tribal consultation) requires grants you may not get.

**DO NOT:**

- Rush into Cherokee Nation contact without relationship foundation
- Promise "partnership" before you have agreement
- Add Cherokee content without any Cherokee input and call it "inclusive"
- Assume adding documents written by Americans about Cherokee counts as "Cherokee perspective"

### RISK #3: Educator Outreach Overpromise

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM
**Potential Blowback:**

- You promise "500+ teachers" and deliver 50
- You create lesson plans no one uses
- TEA/TCSS partnership doesn't materialize and you've announced it

**The Facts:**

- Educator Outreach report assumes $78,400/year budget
- Your actual budget is probably <$5K
- Webinar series, conference presentations, pilot districts all require significant staff time

**Safe Path Forward:**

1. **Do what's free first:** Submit Evidence Room URLs to teacher resource databases
2. **Create ONE excellent lesson plan** (not a whole curriculum) and see if it gets traction
3. **Don't announce partnership until partnership exists** - Contact TEA, gauge interest, then decide
4. **Modest goal:** "If 50 teachers use our Evidence Room in 2026, that's a success"

**DO NOT:**

- Commit to 12 webinars per year with current staffing
- Announce partnership goals before confirming partner interest
- Create elaborate curriculum no one asked for

### RISK #4: Historical Accuracy Claims Under Scrutiny

**Risk Level:** LOW-MEDIUM
**Potential Blowback:**

- Academic historians fact-check your documents and find errors
- Source URLs are broken or point to wrong documents
- "Verified" claims can't be substantiated

**The Facts:**

- Fact-Check Report identified 8 issues (4 critical, 4 moderate)
- Your 87% verified rate is actually excellent for a state historic site
- The Jackson document is the main vulnerability

**Safe Path Forward:**

1. **Fix the 4 critical errors before launch** (3 hours work)
2. **Fix broken URLs** (American State Papers redirects need updating)
3. **Be transparent about verification levels** - Your tiered system (verified/single-source/disputed) is the right approach
4. **Invite academic review** - Ask UT or ETSU history department to review collection. Finding errors privately is better than publicly.

**DO NOT:**

- Launch with known critical errors unfixed
- Claim "verified" status for documents that aren't verified
- Ignore the URL problems (they make you look sloppy)

### RISK #5: SEO/Marketing Hype Disappointment

**Risk Level:** LOW
**Potential Blowback:**

- You expect 5-15x traffic increase and get 2x
- Revenue projections ($1,000-2,700/year) don't materialize
- You spend 40-50 hours on SEO and see minimal impact

**The Facts:**

- SEO projections are reasonable but uncertain
- Your baseline traffic is low, so percentage increases sound impressive but absolute numbers may be modest
- Most historic site SEO improvements take 6-12 months to show results

**Safe Path Forward:**

1. **Do Phase 1 SEO (already done)** - Citation boxes and meta descriptions are complete
2. **Track actual metrics** - Set up Google Analytics baseline before launch
3. **Realistic expectations** - If you go from 500 to 1,500 visitors/month, that's good
4. **Don't over-invest in Phase 2-3** until you see Phase 1 results

**DO NOT:**

- Report projected metrics as actual metrics
- Spend 40-50 hours on SEO before seeing if Phase 1 worked
- Promise board that traffic will "5-15x" (promise "meaningful increase")

## PART 5: FACT-CHECK REALITY CHECK

_Are the reports' claims accurate?_

### Traffic Projections: OPTIMISTIC BUT PLAUSIBLE

**Claim:** SEO improvements could yield 5-15x traffic increase
**Reality:** This is the high end of what's possible with excellent execution. More realistic: 2-3x in first year.
**Verdict:** Plan for 2x, be delighted by 5x, don't promise 15x.

### Revenue Impact: MODEST

**Claim:** SEO could drive $1,000-2,700/year in additional revenue
**Reality:** This assumes visitors convert to ticket purchases. Actual conversion rate unknown.
**Verdict:** Don't make budget decisions based on this projection.

### Cherokee Partnership Costs: ACCURATE BUT SCALABLE

**Claim:** Full partnership costs $24K-70K across 3 phases
**Reality:** These are real costs for professional-level work. But Phase 1 ($2K-5K) is valuable standalone.
**Verdict:** Do Phase 1 if you have $5K. Phases 2-3 require grants.

### Educator Outreach: ASPIRATIONAL

**Claim:** 500+ teachers using Evidence Room by end of 2026
**Reality:** This requires significant outreach effort and assumes TEA/TCSS partnership.
**Verdict:** 50 teachers is a realistic first-year goal without major investment.

### Publication Readiness: ACCURATE

**Claim:** 95% ready to publish (36 of 38 documents)
**Reality:** This is well-documented in the report. The two documents needing work are minor fixes.
**Verdict:** This is the one projection you can trust completely.

### Cherokee Profile Completion: UNDERESTIMATED TIME

**Claim:** 60-80 hours remaining for 39 profiles
**Reality:** That's 1.5-2 hours per profile. Actual quality work takes 2-3 hours per profile.
**Verdict:** Plan for 100+ hours if doing all 39 to the standard of the first 4.
