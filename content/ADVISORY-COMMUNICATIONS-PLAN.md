## PART 1: CORE MESSAGING FRAMEWORK

### What Rocky Mount Can Safely Say

#### Message #1: "Where Tennessee's Government Began"

**Status:** SAFE - Well-documented

**Evidence:**

- William Blount appointed governor: Verified
- Rocky Mount as territorial seat: Documented
- 1790-1792 operations: Primary sources exist

**Talking Points:**

- "Rocky Mount served as the first capital of the Southwest Territory."
- "William Blount established Tennessee's territorial government here."
- "This is where federal governance west of the Appalachians began."

**Media Training:** Cody can say this confidently. Evidence Room backs it up.

---

#### Message #2: "Evidence, Not Claims"

**Status:** SAFE - Differentiator

**Value Proposition:**

- "Most historic sites make claims. We show evidence."
- "Evidence Room contains 38 primary documents proving our history."
- "You don't have to trust us. You can verify us."

**Why This Works:**

- Positions Rocky Mount as transparent
- Sets expectations (we show sources)
- Protects against challenges (we already admitted gaps)

**Media Training:** Use this when asked about credibility.

---

#### Message #3: "A Fuller Story"

**Status:** SAFE WITH CAVEAT - Requires Cherokee partnership

**What to Say:**

- "We're working to represent all voices from 1790s Tennessee."
- "Evidence Room includes Cherokee treaty documents and will expand to include Cherokee perspectives."
- "We acknowledge gaps in the historical record and are actively seeking to fill them."

**What NOT to Say:**

- "We tell the complete story" (we don't)
- "Both sides represented equally" (not yet)
- "Comprehensive Cherokee perspective" (not until partnership is real)

**Media Training:** Be honest about what we DON'T have yet.

### Messages to AVOID Until Fixed

#### ❌ Andrew Jackson Visit

**Status:** DISPUTED - High risk

**Problem:**

- Zero primary sources
- Buildings too new (1826-1830, not 1788)
- Geographic inefficiency
- Better claim elsewhere (Jonesborough)

**What You're Currently Saying:**

- Tours mention Jackson stayed here
- Marketing may suggest Jackson connection

**What You SHOULD Say:**

- "Historical tradition suggests Jackson may have passed through this area in 1788, though the buildings visitors see were constructed in the 1820s-1830s."
- "The Christopher Taylor house in Jonesborough has stronger documented evidence of Jackson's lodging."
- "We're transparent about which claims have primary source evidence and which are based on tradition."

**Risk If You Don't Fix This:**

- Journalist fact-checks
- Academic calls out anachronism
- Visitors feel misled
- Credibility damaged across ALL claims

**Recommendation:** Update tour scripts NOW. Add caveat to all Jackson mentions. Don't feature Jackson in launch marketing.

---

#### ❌ "Complete" Cherokee Representation

**Status:** INCOMPLETE - High cultural risk

**Problem:**

- Zero Cherokee-authored documents currently
- Cherokee are SUBJECTS, not AUTHORS
- No Cherokee Nation consultation yet
- Interpretive essays written by project team, not Cherokee scholars

**What You're Currently Planning:**

- Social media: "42 Cherokee voices" campaign
- Marketing: Positioned as inclusive history

**The Cultural Landmine:**

- Cherokee Nation sees coverage
- Responds: "You're telling OUR story without consulting US"
- Media picks up: "Museum Claims Cherokee Voices, But Cherokee Weren't Involved"

**What You SHOULD Do:**

1. Contact Cherokee Nation BEFORE launch
2. Delay "Cherokee Voices" campaign until partnership exists
3. Frame current content as "U.S. documents about Cherokee relations" (accurate)
4. Promise: "We're actively seeking Cherokee-authored documents and partnership"

## PART 3: CRISIS SCENARIOS & RESPONSES

### Crisis #1: "Your Jackson Claim Is False"

**Trigger:**

- Journalist fact-checks Jackson visit
- Dendrochronology expert points out 1826-1830 construction
- Academic writes critical article

**How It Plays Out:**

- "Rocky Mount Claims Jackson Stayed in Buildings That Didn't Exist Yet"
- Social media picks it up
- Credibility questioned across ALL claims

**Prevention:**

1. Fix tour scripts before launch
2. Add caveat to all Jackson mentions
3. Evidence Room document clearly marks as "disputed tradition"
4. Don't feature Jackson in marketing

**Response If It Happens:**

**Immediate (Day 1):**

```
"We appreciate the fact-checking. You're right that the current structures
date to the 1820s-1830s. We're updating our tour scripts and interpretive
materials to clarify that while Jackson may have visited the property in
1788, the buildings visitors see were constructed decades later. The
Christopher Taylor house in Jonesborough has stronger documented evidence
of Jackson's lodging. We're committed to transparency and accuracy."
```

**Follow-Up (Day 2-3):**

- Update website
- Update tour scripts
- Post transparency statement
- Thank fact-checkers

**Long-Term:**

- Use as example of "Evidence, Not Claims" commitment
- "We fixed our mistake. That's what transparent history looks like."

---

### Crisis #2: Cherokee Nation Criticism

**Trigger:**

- Cherokee Nation sees Evidence Room coverage
- Notices zero Cherokee-authored documents
- Notices Cherokee people are SUBJECTS, not AUTHORS
- Public statement: "They're telling our story without us"

**How It Plays Out:**

- Media: "Museum Claims Cherokee Voices, But Cherokee Weren't Consulted"
- Cherokee community backlash
- Educators question using Evidence Room
- Academic credibility damaged

**Prevention:**

1. Contact Cherokee Nation BEFORE launch
2. Don't launch "Cherokee Voices" campaign until partnership real
3. Frame current content as "U.S. documents about Cherokee relations"
4. Acknowledge gaps publicly

**Response If It Happens:**

**Immediate (Day 1):**

```
"The Cherokee Nation is absolutely right. We currently have U.S. government
documents about Cherokee relations, but we lack Cherokee-authored documents.
This is a gap we take seriously. We've been researching Cherokee-authored
documents and planned to reach out to Cherokee Nation for partnership.
We should have done this before launch. We're pausing our 'Cherokee Voices'
campaign and initiating formal consultation with Cherokee Nation immediately.
We apologize for proceeding without proper tribal consultation."
```

**Follow-Up (Week 1):**

- Contact Cherokee Nation formally
- Offer partnership (not consultation)
- Compensation for expertise
- Cherokee Nation review authority

**Long-Term:**

- Transparent about partnership process
- Regular updates on Cherokee document acquisition
- Cherokee Nation co-authors interpretive content
- Model for how museums fix representation gaps

## PART 4: MEDIA TRAINING FOR CODY

### What Journalists Will Ask

#### Question 1: "How do you know this is where Tennessee's government began?"

**Good Answer:**
"We have primary sources. William Blount's letters describe operating from Rocky Mount. Federal documents show this as the territorial seat. You can verify every claim in Evidence Room—that's why we built it."

**Bad Answer:**
"Everyone knows this" or "It's in the history books" or "Trust us"

---

#### Question 2: "What about Andrew Jackson?"

**Good Answer:**
"Historical tradition suggests Jackson may have passed through this area in 1788, though we acknowledge the buildings visitors see were constructed in the 1820s-1830s. The Christopher Taylor house in Jonesborough has stronger documented evidence of Jackson's lodging."

**Bad Answer:**
"Jackson definitely stayed here" or "We have proof" or defensive deflection

---

#### Question 3: "Why aren't Cherokee voices represented?"

**Good Answer:**
"That's a gap we're actively addressing. We currently have U.S. government documents about Cherokee relations. We're researching Cherokee-authored documents and seeking partnership with Cherokee Nation to ensure authentic representation. We acknowledge what we don't have and we're working to fix it."

**Bad Answer:**
"We have Cherokee content" (no, you have content ABOUT Cherokee) or "That's not our focus"

---

#### Question 4: "Are these claims peer-reviewed?"

**Good Answer:**
"Evidence Room documents are sourced from established archives with full citations. We're partnering with universities for scholarly review and welcome academic scrutiny. Transparency is our standard—if scholars find issues, we'll address them."

**Bad Answer:**
"We're not academics" or "It's good enough" or "Museums don't need peer review"

---

#### Question 5: "How is this different from other historic sites?"

**Good Answer:**
"Most historic sites make claims. We show evidence. Every major assertion about Rocky Mount is backed by a primary document you can verify. We acknowledge gaps and we're transparent about what we don't know. That's rare in public history."

**Bad Answer:**
"We're the best" or comparisons that make others look bad

---

### Hard Questions Practice

**Scenario:** Journalist pushes back

**Journalist:** "But dendrochronology shows these buildings are from the 1820s. How can Jackson have stayed here in 1788?"

**Cody's Response:**
"You're right. The current structures date to the 1820s-1830s. If Jackson visited in 1788, it would have been in earlier buildings that no longer exist. We're clarifying that in our interpretation. Thank you for the fact-check—that's exactly the kind of scrutiny Evidence Room invites."

**Key:** Don't get defensive. Acknowledge. Correct. Thank.
