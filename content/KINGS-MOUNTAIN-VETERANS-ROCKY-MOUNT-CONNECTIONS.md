# King's Mountain Veterans with Rocky Mount and Southwest Territory Connections

**Research Report**
**Date:** January 30, 2026
**Subject:** King's Mountain veterans (October 7, 1780) who later lived near Rocky Mount or served in territorial government

---

## Executive Summary

This report identifies King's Mountain veterans who later lived in the Rocky Mount area (Washington/Sullivan counties) or served in the Southwest Territory government (1790-1796). The Battle of King's Mountain (October 7, 1780) was a pivotal Revolutionary War engagement where approximately 900-1,000 "Overmountain Men" defeated British Loyalist forces in South Carolina. Many of these veterans returned to the Watauga settlements near Rocky Mount and later held positions in the territorial government established by William Blount.

**Key Finding:** Rocky Mount served as a critical supply point for King's Mountain forces before the battle, and later became the territorial capital where several King's Mountain veterans held official positions.

---

## Research Methodology

This research utilized:

1. **Primary Sources:**
   - Revolutionary War pension applications (1818, 1832 Acts)
   - Muster rolls and rosters from King's Mountain
   - Washington County magistrate and militia officer records
   - Southwest Territory appointment records

2. **Secondary Sources:**
   - Bobby Gilmer Moss, _The Patriots of Kings Mountain_ (1990)
   - Lyman C. Draper, _King's Mountain and Its Heroes_
   - Tennessee Encyclopedia entries
   - Digitized genealogical records

3. **Limitations:**
   - Few military records are extant from the battle
   - Many pension applications were filed 38-52 years after service
   - Not all veterans' post-war residences are documented

## King's Mountain Veterans with Confirmed Rocky Mount/Territorial Connections

### 1. JOHN SEVIER (1745-1815)

**Service at King's Mountain:**

- Rank: Colonel, Washington District Regiment
- Role: Commander of Washington County militia forces
- Proof: Multiple primary sources including pension applications, historical accounts

**Connection to Rocky Mount Area:**

- Lived in Washington County (now Tennessee) throughout the 1780s-1790s
- Signer of 1776 Watauga Petition for creation of Washington County
- Major landowner in the region

**Territorial Government Role:**

- Appointed Brigadier General of Washington District Militia by Governor William Blount (1790)
- Member of U.S. Congress representing North Carolina (June 16, 1790 - March 3, 1791)
- Appointed Brigadier General of militia for Washington District (1791)
- First Governor of Tennessee after statehood (1796)

**Documentation:**

- Tennessee Encyclopedia: "Sevier, John"
- House of Representatives History & Archives biographical entry
- Multiple contemporary letters and official records

**Service at King's Mountain:**

- Rank: Sergeant/Militiaman

### 7. WILLIAM COBB SR. (c.1730-c.1802)

**Service at King's Mountain:**

- Role: Civilian supporter and supplier
- Contribution: Supplied the Overmountain Men with gunpowder, horses, blankets, and food

**Connection to Rocky Mount:**

- Owner of Rocky Mount (The Fork)

**Territorial Government Role:**

- Served continuously as magistrate except during State of Franklin period
- Hosted Governor William Blount at Rocky Mount (1790-1792)
- Rocky Mount served as first capital of Southwest Territory

**Later Life:**

- Moved to Beans Station in 1795
- Continued public service in new location

**Documentation:**

**Documentation:**

### 9. ISAAC SHELBY (1750-1826)

**Service at King's Mountain:**

- Rank: Colonel
- Unit: Commander of militia forces
- Role: Key leader at the battle; one of the principal commanders
- Proof: Extensive primary documentation including military reports, pension applications, and historical accounts

**Connection to Rocky Mount Area:**

- Served as commissioner in 1783 to supervise laying off land south of Cumberland River (Middle Tennessee)
- Three commissioners appointed: Absalom Tatom, Isaac Shelby, and Anthony Bledsoe
- In September 1780, assembled with William Campbell and John Sevier at Sycamore Shoals (near Rocky Mount area)

**Territorial Government Role:**

- After creation of Southwest Territory, Shelby moved focus to Kentucky
- First Governor of Kentucky (1792, again in 1812)
- Continued to work on Tennessee/Kentucky border issues

**Later Connection to Region:**

- In 1818, he, Andrew Jackson, and others negotiated the "Jackson Purchase"
- This removed control of western districts of Kentucky and Tennessee from Chickasaw Nation

**Documentation:**

- Tennessee Encyclopedia entry
- Kentucky historical records
- Contemporary correspondence and military records

**Note:** While Isaac Shelby was a key King's Mountain veteran and maintained connections to Tennessee, he moved to Kentucky by the time the Southwest Territory was established. Shelby County, Tennessee is named in his honor.

## Rocky Mount's Role as Supply Point

### Pre-Battle (September 1780)

William Cobb's home at Rocky Mount served as a critical gathering and supply point for the Overmountain Men before the Battle of King's Mountain:

**Historical Documentation:**
"The Kings Mountain soldiers met at William Cobb's home in what was known as The Fork, where they were fed and many mounted on horses furnished by William Cobb, then marched up the Watauga River to Pharaoh Cobb's home where they were provided with food."

**Supplies Provided:**

- Gunpowder
- Horses for mounting soldiers
- Blankets
- Food provisions

**Significance:**
Rocky Mount was strategically located on the route from the Watauga settlements to King's Mountain in South Carolina. William Cobb's support was crucial to the success of the expedition.

**Source:** [Rocky Mount - Sullivan County TN Genealogy](https://tngenweb.org/sullivan/rocky-mount/)

## Rocky Mount as Territorial Capital (1790-1792)

### Governor Blount's Establishment at Rocky Mount

Ten years after supplying the King's Mountain expedition, Rocky Mount became the first capital of the Southwest Territory:

**Timeline:**

- **October 11, 1790:** Governor William Blount arrived at Rocky Mount
- **October 20, 1790:** Blount wrote letter describing the house with "Glass Windows" and "comfortable accommodations"
- **October 23, 1790:** Blount assembled local gentry to announce territorial organization
- **1790-1792:** Rocky Mount served as seat of territorial government

**Territorial Activities at Rocky Mount:**

- Blount met with territorial judges
- Negotiated treaties with Cherokee leaders
- Appointed officials to territorial positions
- Built governmental framework for future state of Tennessee

Several King's Mountain veterans were among those appointed to territorial positions during this period.

## Territorial Government Appointments (1790-1796)

### Governor Blount's Appointments of King's Mountain Veterans

When William Blount established the Southwest Territory government, he strategically appointed frontier leaders who had proven themselves during the Revolutionary War:

**Military Appointments:**

- **John Sevier:** Brigadier General of Washington District Militia
- **James Robertson:** Brigadier General of Mero District Militia
- **Landon Carter:** Lieutenant Colonel, promoted to Colonel (1792-93)

**Civil Appointments:**

- **Joseph Hardin:** Representative to First Territorial Assembly (1794)
- **Archibald Roane:** Attorney and Solicitor for Greene County (1790); Territorial Attorney General for Washington District

**Significance:**
These appointments demonstrate Blount's reliance on Revolutionary War veterans, particularly those from the King's Mountain campaign, to establish legitimacy and maintain order in the frontier territory.

## Analysis and Conclusions

### Pattern of Service

This research reveals a clear pattern:

1. **1780:** King's Mountain veterans fought to defend their frontier settlements from British forces
2. **1780s:** Many returned to Washington/Sullivan counties and held local offices
3. **1790:** When Southwest Territory established, proven military leaders were appointed to key positions
4. **1796:** Several became leaders in the new state of Tennessee

### Rocky Mount's Strategic Importance

Rocky Mount served dual strategic roles:

**Military (1780):**

- Supply depot for King's Mountain expedition
- Gathering point for Overmountain Men
- Provisioning station for forces heading to battle

**Governmental (1790-1792):**

- First territorial capital
- Site of official appointments and treaty negotiations
- Center of territorial administration

### Continuity of Leadership

The same families and individuals who defended the frontier in 1780 were entrusted with governing it in 1790:

- **Sevier family:** John (Colonel at King's Mountain → Brigadier General → First Governor)
- **Valentine Sevier:** (Sergeant at King's Mountain → continued local service)
- **William Cobb:** (Supplier for King's Mountain → Territorial capital host)
- **Joseph Hardin:** (Officer at King's Mountain → Territorial Assemblyman)

### Significance for Rocky Mount Interpretation

For Rocky Mount State Historic Site, these connections demonstrate:

1. The site's importance predates the territorial period
2. The same community leaders served in multiple capacities over time
3. Revolutionary War service credentials were essential for territorial appointments
4. Rocky Mount was central to both military and political development of the region

## Appendices

### Appendix A: King's Mountain Battle Overview

**Date:** October 7, 1780
**Location:** Near present-day Blacksburg, South Carolina
**Forces:**

- Patriot: Approximately 900-1,000 Overmountain Men
- Loyalist: Approximately 1,000 Provincial troops under Major Patrick Ferguson

**Key Patriot Commanders:**

- Colonel William Campbell (overall commander)
- Colonel John Sevier (Washington County, NC)
- Colonel Isaac Shelby
- Colonel Benjamin Cleveland
- Colonel Joseph McDowell
- Colonel James Johnston

**Result:** Decisive Patriot victory; Major Ferguson killed; approximately 290 Loyalists killed, 163 wounded, 668 captured

**Significance:**

- Turned tide of war in the South
- Thomas Jefferson: "turned the tide of success which terminated the Revolutionary War"
- Forced British General Cornwallis to abandon invasion of North Carolina
- Demonstrated effectiveness of frontier militia

**Sources:**

- [Battle of Kings Mountain - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Kings_Mountain)
- [Kings Mountain - American Battlefield Trust](https://www.battlefields.org/learn/revolutionary-war/battles/kings-mountain)

### Appendix B: Southwest Territory Overview

**Official Name:** Territory of the United States South of the River Ohio
**Existed:** May 26, 1790 - June 1, 1796
**Capital:** Rocky Mount (1790-1792), then Knoxville (1792-1796)
**Governor:** William Blount (only governor)

**Creation:**

- Established by Act of Congress following North Carolina's cession of western lands
- Organized under Northwest Ordinance principles

**Government Structure:**

- Governor (appointed by President)
- Three judges (appointed)
- Territorial legislature (when population reached 5,000 free males)
- Two brigadier generals of militia

**Key Events:**

- 1790: Blount arrives at Rocky Mount
- 1791: Treaty of Holston negotiated with Cherokee
- 1792: Capital moves to Knoxville
- 1794: First Territorial Assembly convenes
- 1796: Becomes State of Tennessee

**Sources:**

- [Southwest Territory - Tennessee Encyclopedia](https://tennesseeencyclopedia.net/entries/southwest-territory/)
- [Southwest Territory - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwest_Territory)

### Appendix C: Washington County, NC/TN Overview

**Washington County, North Carolina** (later Tennessee):

- **Created:** 1777 (from western North Carolina lands)
- **Original Territory:** Covered most of present-day East Tennessee
- **Named For:** George Washington
- **County Seat:** Initially Jonesborough (established 1779)

**Key Facts for This Study:**

- Home to 240 soldiers who fought at King's Mountain
- William Cobb was one of 27 original magistrates (1777)
- Rocky Mount located in Washington County
- Most King's Mountain veterans from this region returned to Washington County

**Later Division:**
As Tennessee grew, Washington County was subdivided to create:

- Sullivan County (1779)
- Greene County (1783)
- Hawkins County (1786)
- And others
