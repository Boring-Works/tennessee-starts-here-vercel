## Primary Sources

### Founding Documents & Treaties

**Treaty of Holston (1791)**
> "Treaty of Holston between the United States of America and the Cherokee Nation." July 2, 1791. National Archives, Record Group 11. Founders Online, National Archives. https://founders.archives.gov.

*Reference Library IDs:* trt-001, trt-004, trt-005, trt-006, trt-007, trt-008, trt-009, trt-010, trt-011, trt-012, trt-013, trt-014, trt-015, trt-016

*Key Claims Supported:*
- Treaty signed July 2, 1791 at White's Fort (not Rocky Mount)
- 41-42 Cherokee chiefs signed
- Seven principal chiefs: Hanging Maw, Bloody Fellow, John Watts, Doublehead, Black Fox, Standing Turkey, The Boots
- $1,000 initial annuity, increased to $1,500 (Feb 1792)
- George Washington ratified November 11, 1791

**Short Citation:** Treaty of Holston, July 2, 1791 (National Archives)

### Cherokee Diplomacy Sources

**Knox, Henry to George Washington. January 17, 1792.**

*Reference Library IDs:* trt-017, trt-018, trt-019, trt-020, trt-022, trt-023, trt-024

*Key Claims Supported:*

**Quotable Passage (Little Turkey's Warning):**

## Archaeological Sources

**Dendrochronology Study (2006-2007)**
> Grissino-Mayer, Henri D., and Saskia L. van de Gevel. "Dendrochronological Dating of Rocky Mount Historic Site." Report prepared for Tennessee Historical Commission, 2007.

*Reference Library IDs:* con-001, con-005, ppl-005

*Key Claims Supported:*
- Current main house (Cobb House) built 1826-1828
- Dining Room structure built 1829-1830
- Michael Massengill (grandson) built current structures

**CRITICAL FINDING:** The current Cobb House was NOT standing during Blount's governorship (1790-1792). An earlier structure existed on the property.

**Short Citation:** Grissino-Mayer & van de Gevel, Dendrochronology Study, 2007 (Tennessee Historical Commission)

## Source Hierarchy

When claims conflict, use this priority:

1. **Primary Sources** (letters, treaties, official records) - Highest authority
2. **Archaeological Evidence** (dendrochronology, excavations) - Scientific verification
3. **Scholarly Secondary Sources** (Tennessee Encyclopedia, academic papers) - Informed interpretation
4. **Oral Tradition** - Use with "according to tradition" qualifier
