## 2. Lisa Bennett

**Status:** ✅ VERIFIED

### Current Role

- **Living History Interpreter**, Sycamore Shoals State Historic Park, Elizabethton, TN
- Specializes in first-person portrayal of **Mary Patton**

### Programs

- **"Keep Yer' Powder Dry"** - Signature program at Sycamore Shoals
- Featured presenter at Overmountain Victory Trail Celebration (annual)
- Living History Weekend participant

### Previous Rocky Mount Appearance

- **March 2019:** Presented "Mary Patton: Colonial Patriot" at Rocky Mount's Massengill Museum for Women's History Month

### The Historical Figure: Mary Patton (1751-1836)

- Born December 15, 1751 in England
- Learned gunpowder making from father David McKeehan (Scottish powder maker)
- Provided **500+ pounds of gunpowder** to Overmountain Men at Sycamore Shoals (September 1780)
- Her powder was essential to victory at Kings Mountain (October 7, 1780)
- Jefferson, Washington, and Gates called Kings Mountain "the turning point of the American Revolution"

### Rocky Mount Synergy

**Direct artifact connection:** Rocky Mount holds one of Mary Patton's original gunpowder kettles (two others at Sycamore Shoals). Both sites commemorate the Overmountain campaign - Sycamore Shoals was the muster point, Rocky Mount's Cobb family supplied horses, blankets, and provisions.

**Lecture:** "Powder for Kings Mountain" (April 18, 2026) - First-person interpretation
