# Press & Media Strategy: Evidence Room Launch

**Rocky Mount State Historic Site**
**Prepared:** January 30, 2026
**Launch Window:** Spring 2026 (aligns with Tennessee 230 / America 250)

---

## Executive Summary

The Evidence Room is a **scholarly digital archive** that positions Rocky Mount as the definitive source on Tennessee territorial history. This media strategy targets three audiences:

1. **National/Academic Media** — Historians, history teachers, scholarly press
2. **State/Regional Media** — Tennessee news outlets, tourism publications
3. **Specialized Media** — Podcasts, documentaries, academic journals

**Key Messaging:** Rocky Mount isn't just a museum. The Evidence Room proves through primary documents that this ground is where Tennessee's government began—and makes that evidence available to the world.

**Success Metrics:**

- 50+ media placements (national, state, local)
- 15+ podcast features / 5+ documentary partnerships
- 10+ academic journal / publication mentions
- 500K+ reach in first 90 days

```

```

#### BODY (300 words)

**PINEY FLATS, TN** — The Evidence Room isn't just a museum digitizing old papers. It's a demonstration of how AI and careful historical methodology can work together—and how it should.

The archive employs:

**Natural Language Processing** to extract names, places, and dates from historical documents automatically, making searches intuitive even for users unfamiliar with 18th-century spelling and handwriting.

**Entity Linking** to connect documents across the collection—when a user reads about "William Blount," the system automatically surfaces every document mentioning him, every person he corresponded with, and every treaty he signed.

**Semantic Search** allowing researchers to find documents by concept ("Cherokee diplomacy") rather than keyword ("Cherokee").

**Structured Metadata** ensuring every document carries verification information: who digitized it, which repository owns it, when scholars last reviewed it, whether academic partners have analyzed it.

"We're treating historical documents the way libraries treat academic articles," said [Director Name]. "Everything is indexed. Everything is discoverable. Everything carries metadata about its authority."

The system architecture is **open and documented**, with full source code available for other museums and archives to adapt.

**Technical Features:**

- Full-text search across 200+ documents
- Timeline visualization of events by month and year
- Network graph showing connections between people and documents
- Export functionality in multiple formats (BibTeX, Dublin Core, plain text)
- API access for researchers and academic institutions

"Most digital archives are black boxes," said [Technical Partner]. "You can search them, but you can't understand how. The Evidence Room is transparent about its methods. Researchers can audit our work."

The project demonstrates that **historical scholarship and digital innovation** aren't separate domains—they're complementary.

- Journal of Digital Humanities
- Digital Humanities Quarterly
- Archives and Records

**Paper Idea:** "Scholarly Digital Archives as Authority: The Evidence Room Case Study"

```
Abstract:
Museums face a credibility challenge: visitors and researchers want proof,
not claims. This paper documents how Rocky Mount State Historic Site built
an open, transparent archive of 200+ primary documents, making every historical
assertion verifiable. We discuss:

- Schema design for historical documents
- Metadata standards for verification and authority
- User research (how scholars, teachers, and visitors search archives)
- Impact on institutional positioning and public trust
- Open-source approach enabling other institutions to replicate the model

This work demonstrates how digital archives function as institutional
authority-building tools, not just content repositories.
```
