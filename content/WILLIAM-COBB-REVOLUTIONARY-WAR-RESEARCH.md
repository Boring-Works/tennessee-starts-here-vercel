# William Cobb's Revolutionary War Service

## Research Report

**Subject:** William Cobb Sr. (c. 1730-1803)
**Research Date:** January 30, 2026
**Location:** Rocky Mount, Sullivan County (Washington County, NC Territory)
**Research Focus:** Revolutionary War service 1775-1781

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

William Cobb Sr. served the Revolutionary cause primarily in a **logistical and civil capacity** rather than as a combat soldier. His documented service includes:

1. **Material Support for King's Mountain Campaign** (September-October 1780)
2. **Civil Service** as Washington County, NC Magistrate (1777)
3. **Financial Service** as Judge and Viewer of Currency of the Realm (1780)
4. **Family Participation** - Five Cobb household members answered the call to arms for King's Mountain

**Note on King's Mountain Participation:** While roster sources list William Cobb Sr. and William Cobb Jr. as participants in the Battle of King's Mountain (October 7, 1780), primary source documentation of their direct combat role has not been located. Their participation appears confirmed through multiple secondary sources, but detailed military service records remain elusive.

## DOCUMENTED REVOLUTIONARY WAR SERVICE

### 1. KING'S MOUNTAIN CAMPAIGN (September-October 1780)

#### Material Support Provided

According to Rocky Mount State Historic Site records:

> "William and Pharaoh supplied the Overmountain Men with gunpowder, horses, blankets, and food for their journey to the Battle of King's Mountain."

**Specific Contributions:**

- Gunpowder
- Horses for mounting soldiers
- Blankets
- Food provisions
- Use of Rocky Mount as a gathering/staging point

#### Family Members Who Answered the Call

Five members of the Cobb household participated:

1. **William Cobb Sr.** (patriarch)
2. **William Cobb Jr.** (son)
3. **Pharaoh Cobb** (son) - Served under Col. Isaac Shelby
4. **Jerry Cobb** (family member)
5. **Arthur Cobb** (family member)

**Source:** [Rocky Mount State Historic Site](https://www.rockymountmuseum.com/our-history), [Sullivan County TN Genealogy](https://tngenweb.org/sullivan/rocky-mount/)

#### Rocky Mount as Strategic Location

According to multiple sources:

> "The Kings Mountain soldiers met at William Cobb's in The Fork where they were fed and many of them mounted on horses furnished by William Cobb, then marched to Pharaoh Cobb's home where they were provided with food, and Pharaoh Cobb went with them."

**Timeline:**

- **September 25, 1780** - Overmountain Men mustered at Sycamore Shoals
- **September 24, 1780** - Virginia forces under William Campbell gathered at Rocky Mount
- **October 7, 1780** - Battle of King's Mountain

**Source:** [WikiTree - William Cobb Sr.](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Cobb-3509)

### 3. CIVIL SERVICE DURING THE REVOLUTIONARY WAR

#### Washington County Magistrate (1777)

**Appointment:** 1777, when North Carolina legislature changed Washington District to Washington County, NC

**Service:** William Cobb served as one of twenty-seven original magistrates appointed for Washington County, North Carolina. He continued in this role except during the State of Franklin period (1784-1789).

**Additional Service:** Commission member charged with laying out the town of Jonesborough.

**Source:** [Rocky Mount - Tennessee Encyclopedia](https://tennesseeencyclopedia.net/entries/rocky-mount/)

#### Judge and Viewer of Currency of the Realm (1780)

**Position:** One of the "Judges and Viewers of Currency of the Realm" for Washington County in 1780.

**Context:** This position involved inspecting and validating currency during a period of significant monetary instability in the Revolutionary economy.

**Source:** [WikiTree - William Cobb Sr.](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Cobb-3509)

## HISTORICAL CONTEXT

### The Overmountain Men Campaign

**Background:** In September 1780, British Major Patrick Ferguson threatened to march over the mountains and "lay waste the country with fire and sword" if the frontier settlers did not cease their opposition to British rule.

**Response:** Frontier militia leaders Isaac Shelby (Sullivan County) and John Sevier (Washington County) issued a call to arms.

**Muster:** September 25, 1780, at Sycamore Shoals

- Isaac Shelby: 240 men (Sullivan County, NC)
- John Sevier: 240 men (Washington County, NC)
- William Campbell: 400 men (Washington County, VA)
- Charles McDowell: 160 men (Burke and Rutherford Counties, NC)

**Total Force:** Over 1,100 volunteers

**Battle:** October 7, 1780 - American victory at King's Mountain, SC

**Strategic Importance:** Considered a turning point in the Revolutionary War's Southern Campaign.

**Sources:**

- [NC DNCR - King's Mountain Battleground](https://www.dncr.nc.gov/blog/2024/01/18/kings-mountain-battleground-o-5)
- [Tennessee State Guard Returns to Sycamore Shoals](https://www.tn.gov/military/news/2015/8/19/tennessee-state-guard-returns-to-sycamore-shoals.html)

### William Cobb's Settlement and Standing

**Migration:** 1769 from North Carolina (Isle of Wight County, Virginia origin)

**Settlement:** Built Rocky Mount on the Watauga River between 1769-1772 (traditional dating)

**Note on Construction Date:** Recent dendrochronological (tree-ring) study challenges the traditional 1770-1772 construction date, suggesting the current standing structure was built 1827-1830 by William Cobb's grandson, Michael Massengill. This does not negate William Cobb's settlement in the area, but indicates the current building may be a later reconstruction.

**Source:** [Construction History of the Rocky Mount Historic Site - Tree-Ring and Documentary Evidence](https://www.academia.edu/191594/)

**Family:** Married Barsheba Whitehead (his cousin)

**Prominence:** One of the first settlers and most distinguished citizens of the territory

**Post-War Service:** Rocky Mount served as the first capitol of the Southwest Territory (1790-1792) under Governor William Blount

**Death:** 1803 in Knox County, Tennessee (moved to Knoxville area in 1795)

**Sources:**

- [WikiTree - William Cobb Sr.](https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Cobb-3509)
- [Sullivan County TN Genealogy](https://tngenweb.org/sullivan/rocky-mount/)

## CONCLUSIONS

### Documented Service Summary

William Cobb Sr.'s Revolutionary War service is **documented but not comprehensively recorded** in surviving military records. The evidence indicates:

**Confirmed:**

1. Material support for Overmountain Men (gunpowder, horses, blankets, food)
2. Rocky Mount served as gathering/staging point for King's Mountain campaign
3. Listed on Battle of King's Mountain patriot rosters (multiple sources)
4. Civil service as Washington County magistrate (1777)
5. Public service as Judge and Viewer of Currency (1780)
6. Five Cobb household members participated in King's Mountain campaign

**Probable but Undocumented:**

1. Direct participation in the march to King's Mountain
2. Presence at the battle on October 7, 1780
3. Military rank or unit assignment

**Not Located:**

1. Pension application
2. Compiled military service record
3. Bounty land warrant
4. Pay vouchers
5. Contemporary military correspondence

### Character of Service

William Cobb's Revolutionary War service appears to have been primarily **logistical and civil** rather than combat-oriented. As a 50-year-old property owner with significant resources (horses, livestock, provisions), he played a crucial support role that enabled the Overmountain Men's successful campaign.

His civil service as magistrate and currency inspector during the war years demonstrates active participation in maintaining governmental structure and economic stability in the frontier region during the Revolutionary period.

### Significance

William Cobb's contribution to the Revolutionary cause, while not characterized by combat service, was strategically important:

- **Material Support:** Provided essential supplies at a critical moment
- **Strategic Location:** Rocky Mount served as a gathering point and staging area
- **Family Commitment:** Five household members answered the call to arms
- **Civil Leadership:** Maintained governmental functions during wartime
- **Post-War Service:** Rocky Mount became the first territorial capitol
