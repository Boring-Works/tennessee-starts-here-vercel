Henry Knox served as the first Secretary of War under President Washington from 1789 to 1794. In this role, he oversaw Indian affairs and the administration of the western territories. Knox authorized Governor Blount's treaty negotiations with the Cherokee and helped shape federal Indian policy during the critical early years of the republic. The city of Knoxville, Tennessee was named in his honor.
