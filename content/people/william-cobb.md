William Cobb was a prosperous frontier settler whose home, Rocky Mount, served as the first capital of the Southwest Territory from October 1790 to late 1791. His substantial two-story frame house, notable for its glass windows and comfortable accommodations, provided Governor William Blount with headquarters for the territorial government during its formative months. The property's strategic location on the Great Road from Virginia made it an ideal center for the new government.
