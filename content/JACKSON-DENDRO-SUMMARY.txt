ANDREW JACKSON AT ROCKY MOUNT 1788 — DENDROCHRONOLOGY FACT-CHECK SUMMARY
================================================================================

CORE QUESTION: Could Andrew Jackson have stayed at Rocky Mount in 1788?

ANSWER TO BUILDINGS: NO — Physical impossibility
ANSWER TO PROPERTY: POSSIBLY, but unverifiable

================================================================================
KEY EVIDENCE
================================================================================

DENDROCHRONOLOGY (CERTAIN):
- Current main house: Built 1827-1830 (UT study, Grissino-Mayer & van de Gevel 2007)
- Jackson's claimed visit: Spring 1788
- Gap: 38-42 years AFTER the tradition
- Verdict: Cannot have stayed in current house

BUILDER IDENTIFICATION:
- Current house built by: Michael Massengill (William Cobb's grandson)
- This is 40 years after Jackson supposedly lodged with William Cobb

JACKSON'S DOCUMENTED 1788 TIMELINE:
✓ Admitted to NC bar: September 1787
✓ Crossed Appalachians: Spring 1788
✓ In Western District: Spring/Summer 1788
✓ Nashville as district attorney: October 1788
✓ Timeline fits Rocky Mount stay geographically

BUT NO PRIMARY DOCUMENTATION EXISTS:
✗ No letters from Jackson mentioning Rocky Mount
✗ No Cobb family papers mentioning Jackson
✗ No contemporary receipts or tavern records
✗ No diary entries
✗ No 1788 newspaper mentions

SECONDARY SOURCES (Consistent but Unverified):
- Tennessee Encyclopedia
- Miller Center, University of Virginia
- Andrew Jackson's Hermitage
- Library of Congress Andrew Jackson Papers (indexed but claim not documented)

ALL cite "six weeks at Cobb's house, Spring 1788" but none cite PRIMARY EVIDENCE

================================================================================
WILLIAM COBB'S 1788 PROPERTY
================================================================================

DID HE LIVE THERE?
Yes — Confirmed. Cobb owned Rocky Mount site in 1788.

WHAT BUILDING EXISTED?
Unknown but likely. Governor Blount's 1790 description mentions:
- "Substantial two-story [frame/log] house"
- Glass windows (luxury item)
- Suitable for territorial government HQ

KEY QUESTION: Is the 1790 house the same as what Jackson would have seen in 1788?
ANSWER: Unknown. Possibly earlier structure replaced by 1827-1830 house.

WAS IT A LOG HOUSE?
Not specified in primary sources. Document authors infer "log structure"
but Blount calls it "frame house" (could mean frame-construction or timber-framed).

WHAT HAPPENED TO THE 1790 BUILDING?
Three possibilities:
1. Burned/destroyed (common on frontier; no records)
2. Dismantled (logs reused; common practice)
3. Still standing (would have different dendro date but unexamined)

ARCHAEOLOGICAL STATUS: Not investigated. No artifact evidence supports 1788 structures.

================================================================================
THE PHYSICAL IMPOSSIBILITY
================================================================================

Modern visitors see the 1827-1830 house and hear the Jackson story.

TOURISTS THINK: "Jackson stayed in THIS building"
DENDROCHRONOLOGY PROVES: "This building didn't exist in 1788"

IMPLICATION: If Jackson visited, it was in a DIFFERENT BUILDING that no longer stands.

This creates a credibility problem: You're telling a tradition about a building
that didn't exist yet when the event allegedly occurred.

THREE ASSESSMENT OPTIONS
================================================================================

OPTION A: Earlier Structures (WEAK EVIDENCE)
- Theory: Jackson stayed in earlier Cobb house (pre-1827)
- Supporting: Building existed in 1790; Jackson was in region; property suitable
- Against: No primary documentation; no archaeology; no when built/destroyed timeline
- Verdict: Theoretically possible but unverifiable

OPTION B: Tradition Is False (STRONG EVIDENCE)
- Theory: Jackson never visited Rocky Mount
- Supporting: Zero primary documentation; no contemporary mentions; common false traditions
- Against: Multiple sources agree; Jackson was in region; Cobb's was guest house
- Verdict: Plausible but contradicts secondary source consensus

OPTION C: Conflation With Other Location (MEDIUM EVIDENCE)
- Theory: Jackson visited elsewhere (Jonesborough) and tradition got confused
- Supporting: Jonesborough was his actual destination
- Against: No Jonesborough sources claim him; Tennessee Encyclopedia specifically says Rocky Mount
- Verdict: Deserves research but insufficient evidence now
