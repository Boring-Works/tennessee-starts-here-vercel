# Andrew Jackson at Rocky Mount, 1788: Complete Fact-Check Analysis

**Mission:** Verify the "Jackson stayed at Rocky Mount in Spring 1788" claim by examining documented Jackson movements and competing evidence

**Status:** ✓ COMPLETE
**Date:** January 30, 2026
**Finding:** CLAIM IS UNVERIFIED - Lacks primary sources; contradicts dendrochronology; better evidence points to Jonesborough

---

## Quick Answer

### Did Jackson Really Stay at Rocky Mount for Six Weeks in Spring 1788?

**SHORT ANSWER:** Probably not—or at least, there's no evidence to support it.

**WHAT WE KNOW FOR CERTAIN:**

- Jackson admitted to NC bar: September 1787 (documented) ✓
- Jackson in Jonesborough: August 1788 (documented in duel incident) ✓
- Jackson in Nashville: October 1788 (documented in appointment records) ✓

**WHAT'S UNPROVEN:**

- Six-week stay at Rocky Mount (Spring 1788): ZERO primary sources ✗
- Current buildings dating to 1788: DENDROCHRONOLOGY says 1826-1830 ✗
- Why Rocky Mount instead of Jonesborough?: NO DOCUMENTED REASON ✗

**CONCLUSION:** Unverified tradition repeated in secondary sources for 100+ years, but completely lacking in primary documentation. Contradicted by building dates.

## Key Evidence Points

### The Three Fatal Problems

#### 1. ZERO Primary Source Documentation

- No Jackson letters mentioning Rocky Mount
- No Cobb family records
- No contemporary 1788 accounts
- No diary entries
- All evidence is secondary (100+ years old)

**Implication:** Claim cannot be independently verified

#### 2. Physical Impossibility (Dendrochronology)

- UT dendrochronology: Current structures built 1826-1830
- Jackson's claimed visit: 1788
- Gap: 38-42 YEARS before buildings existed
- Built by: Michael Massengill (Cobb's grandson)

**Implication:** Jackson could NOT have stayed in buildings visitors see today

#### 3. Geographic Inefficiency

- Jackson needed Jonesborough (where his legal business was)
- Rocky Mount is 30 miles PAST Jonesborough
- Direct route more efficient
- No documented reason for detour

**Implication:** Route doesn't make logical sense

### The Stronger Alternative

**From Evidence Room itself:** "Christopher Taylor house in Jonesborough has stronger documented evidence"

**Why Jonesborough is Better:**

- Jackson was definitely there in August 1788 (duel incident, documented)
- That's where his legal business was (court license)
- More geographically logical
- More historically sensible

**Gap:** Christopher Taylor house claim should have separate Evidence Room document
