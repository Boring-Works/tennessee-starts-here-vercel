# Evidence Room: Partnership & Collaboration Strategy

**Date:** January 30, 2026
**Prepared For:** Rocky Mount State Historic Site
**Context:** Evidence Room launches with 38 primary sources; partnerships will amplify reach, deepen scholarship, and establish Rocky Mount as a regional authority

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Evidence Room is a unique scholarly asset: a living archive of 1775-1796 primary documents that tell the story of Tennessee's founding through multiple perspectives—Cherokee, federal, enslaved people, settlers, women, and local voices. Strategic partnerships can amplify this resource beyond traditional museum audiences to reach researchers, educators, students, and cultural institutions across the Southeast.

**Partnership Vision:** Position Rocky Mount and Evidence Room as:

- A model for inclusive digital history (Cherokee + settler + enslaved perspectives)
- A scholarly resource for university research and teaching
- A community hub for historical understanding
- A reference authority for East Tennessee history

**Key Partnership Categories:**

1. University partnerships (research, teaching, student engagement)
2. Cherokee Nation collaboration (cultural authority, shared stewardship)
3. Historic site networks (multi-site promotion)
4. Tourism ecosystems (visitor pipelines)
5. Library systems (academic & public reach)
6. Museum/archive associations (scholarly credibility)

**Expected Outcomes (12-24 months):**

- 10+ university courses using Evidence Room
- 5,000+ student exposures annually
- Cherokee Nation co-created content in Evidence Room
- 50+ cross-promoted historic sites
- Integration into 25+ library catalogs
- 200,000+ annual Evidence Room visitors

## PART 2: CHEROKEE NATION COLLABORATION

### 2.1 Strategic Rationale

Evidence Room currently has an imbalance: U.S. government voices (Washington, Jefferson, Blount) dominate. Cherokee sources are limited to treaty documents and referenced quotes. A genuine partnership with Cherokee Nation helps Evidence Room achieve its core mission: **telling the fuller story of 1775-1796 Tennessee**.

**Key Principle:** Cherokee Nation is not a "partner to consult"—they are **co-stewards of interpretation**. This is fundamentally different from extractive partnerships.

**Partnership Goals:**

- Add 4-6 Cherokee-authored or Cherokee-centered documents (18-24 months)
- Create "Cherokee Perspectives" section of Evidence Room
- Establish Cherokee Nation as editorial authority on Cherokee representation
- Enable Cherokee Nation to use Evidence Room for their own education/advocacy

---

### 2.2 Partnership Phases

**Phase 1: Relationship Building (Months 1-2)**

**Objectives:**

- Establish trust and understand Cherokee Nation's priorities
- Identify decision-makers and stakeholders
- Understand their archival holdings and expertise
- Clarify what mutual benefit looks like

**Actions:**

1. **Initial Contact (Cody, with letter of introduction)**
   - From: Rocky Mount Director
   - To: Cherokee Nation of Oklahoma, Department of Culture & History
   - Cc: Regional/Education director
   - Key Points:
     - Acknowledge Cherokee nation's role in 1790s events
     - Explain Evidence Room's current gaps
     - Propose collaboration (not consultation)
     - Ask for meeting to discuss
   - Tone: Respectful, humble about limitations
   - Include: Evidence Room link, 2-3 example documents

2. **Initial Meeting (Video or in-person)**
   - Participants: Cody (Rocky Mount), Cherokee Nation history director/educator
   - Agenda:
     - Walk through Evidence Room (what exists)
     - Discuss imbalances they see
     - Ask: "What do you know that Evidence Room is missing?"
     - Ask: "How can we serve your community's needs?"
     - Ask: "What would meaningful collaboration look like to you?"
   - Output: Understanding of Cherokee Nation's interests + willingness to proceed

3. **Establish Advisory Role**
   - Invite 2-3 Cherokee Nation scholars/educators to serve as "Evidence Room Cherokee Advisors"
   - Commitment: 1 hour/month (review submitted documents, offer feedback)
   - Benefit to them: Early access to new documents, ability to influence direction
   - Compensation: $200/month honorarium + attribution

**Phase 1 Outcome:** Formal partnership agreement + signed advisory board

---

**Phase 2: Content Co-Creation (Months 3-8)**

**Objectives:**

- Identify and acquire Cherokee-centered documents
- Create Cherokee-authored contextual essays
- Pilot "Cherokee Perspectives" section
- Test editorial process with Cherokee Nation

**Actions:**

1. **Document Acquisition (Led by Cherokee advisors)**
   - What's needed:
     - Hanging Maw letters (diplomatic voice)
     - Cherokee delegation speeches (1791-1793)
     - Treaty documents with Cherokee annotations/commentary
     - Oral histories (recorded with permission)
   - Process:
     - Cherokee Nation recommends documents
     - Rocky Mount sources and digitizes
     - Advisors verify accuracy + context
   - Target: 4-6 documents by end of Phase 2

2. **"Cherokee Perspectives" Essays**
   - Format: 1,000-2,000 word contextual essays
   - Authors: Cherokee Nation scholars (3-4 essays)
   - Topics:
     - "Cherokee Diplomacy & the Treaty of Holston"
     - "Who Were the Signatories? Stories Beyond Names"
     - "Oral History & Written Records: Different Truths"
     - "Cherokee Resilience in the Archive"
   - Process:
     - Rocky Mount provides guidelines
     - Cherokee Nation authors
     - Joint review/approval
   - Benefit: Cherokee Nation's voice & authority in Evidence Room

3. **Oral History Pilot (Optional, if capacity)**
   - Record 2-3 Cherokee Nation elders/scholars discussing:
     - Reactions to Evidence Room documents
     - Stories not in the archive
     - What it means to see their ancestors' names
   - Format: 20-30 minute videos
   - Hosted in Evidence Room as supplemental content
   - Benefit: Brings documents "alive" with contemporary perspective

4. **Accessibility Review**
   - Cherokee Nation reviews all new content
   - Feedback on tone, accuracy, representation
   - Approval before publication
   - Built-in mechanism for ongoing correction

**Phase 2 Outcome:** 4-6 new documents + 3-4 contextual essays + "Cherokee Perspectives" section launched

---

**Phase 3: Reciprocal Benefit (Months 9-18)**

**Objectives:**

- Evidence Room becomes resource for Cherokee Nation
- Deepen collaboration based on Phase 2 success
- Expand to other tribal nations (if appropriate)

**Actions:**

1. **Cherokee Nation Education Integration**
   - Provide Cherokee Nation educators with curated "Evidence Room Teaching Kit"
   - Support Cherokee Nation's own education programs (schools, cultural centers)
   - Enable Cherokee Nation to embed Evidence Room in their website
   - Support translation/accessibility (Cherokee language annotations?)

2. **Extended Advisory Board**
   - Expand from 2-3 to 5-6 advisors (include younger voices)
   - Quarterly meetings (1 hour each)
   - Paid consultants (not volunteers)
   - Formal board structure with rotating chair

3. **Co-Marketing**
   - Feature partnership in Evidence Room marketing
   - Cherokee Nation shares Evidence Room in their newsletters/social
   - Joint press release on "Cherokee Perspectives" section launch
   - Possibility: Co-hosted webinar/presentation series

4. **Ongoing Document Identification**
   - Establish process for Cherokee Nation to nominate documents year-round
   - Rocky Mount commits to quarterly review cycle
   - Transparent pipeline: nominated → being researched → approved → published

**Phase 3 Outcome:** Evidence Room becomes known as the authoritative source for inclusive 1790s history; Cherokee Nation actively uses Evidence Room for education

---

**Phase 4: Long-term Model (Year 2+)**

**Objectives:**

- Establish Evidence Room as a model for inclusive archival work
- Explore expansion to other indigenous nations (Chickasaw, Shawnee, Quapaw)
- Formalize partnership in publications and conference presentations

**Actions:**

1. **Chickasaw Nation Engagement (Optional)**
   - Similar partnership to Cherokee Nation
   - Identify Chickasaw-relevant documents (1790s interactions)
   - Support Chickasaw education programs

2. **Published Scholarship**
   - Co-author articles on "Partnership Models for Inclusive Digital History"
   - Present at American Historical Association, Organization of American Historians
   - Case study: "Evidence Room as Model"

3. **Field Building**
   - Share partnership framework with other historic sites
   - Contribute to field discussions on indigenous representation in digital archives

### 2.3 Partnership Agreement Components

**Formal MOU should include:**

- **Content Authority:** Cherokee Nation has final approval on all Cherokee-related materials
- **Attribution:** Clear bylines and credit for Cherokee Nation contributors
- **Compensation:** Hourly rate for advisory work; potential honorariums for essays
- **Access:** Cherokee Nation has early/exclusive access to new Cherokee-related documents
- **Usage Rights:** Cherokee Nation can re-share Evidence Room content for education (non-commercial)
- **Escalation Process:** Mechanism for addressing disagreements (e.g., about interpretation)
- **Term:** 2-year initial, renewable annually with review
- **Termination:** Either party can end with 90 days notice; content remains in Evidence Room

### 3.2 Partnership Model: "Evidence Trail"

**Concept:** Create a digital and physical experience where visitors moving through East Tennessee's historic sites encounter Evidence Room documents relevant to each location.

**Example 1: Sycamore Shoals Historic Area**

- Visitors see exhibit on Treaty of Sycamore Shoals (1775)
- QR code links to Evidence Room's "Treaty Collection"
- Document: Full text of Treaty of Sycamore Shoals
- Historical context essay
- 2-3 related documents (treaty signatories, background)
- Drives interested visitors to Evidence Room for deeper research

**Example 2: Fort Loudon State Historic Area**

- Exhibit on Fort Loudon's role in 1760s-70s
- QR code to Evidence Room documents on federal-Cherokee relations
- Links to specific documents about fort in archives
- Encourages further research

**Example 3: East Tennessee History Center (Knoxville)**

- "Featured Research" in lobby mentions Evidence Room
- Tourist brochure: "Dig Deeper: Evidence Room Online Archive"
- Staff trained to recommend specific documents to interested visitors
- Increases repeat visitors to Evidence Room

**Implementation:**

1. **Phase 1: Partnership Pitch (Feb-March 2026)**
   - Contact 5-7 site directors
   - Elevator pitch: "Free marketing for you; they drive traffic to our exhibits too"
   - Propose: QR code partnership + staff training
   - Cost to partner: Minimal (printing QR codes)

2. **Phase 2: Content Curation (April-May 2026)**
   - Rocky Mount curator identifies Evidence Room documents relevant to each site
   - Create curated "collections" (e.g., "Fort Loudon Documents")
   - Provide to partners with context essays

3. **Phase 3: Implementation (June 2026)**
   - Print materials with QR codes
   - Train site staff on Evidence Room
   - Soft launch at 2-3 sites

4. **Phase 4: Expansion (July 2026+)**
   - Expand to 5-10 additional sites
   - Establish "Historic Sites Partner" badge on Evidence Room
   - Cross-promote in annual guide for East Tennessee

**Expected Results:**

- 3,000-5,000 visitor referrals from historic sites (Year 1)
- 50+ sites in network (Year 2)
- Branded as "The Evidence Trail" (regional initiative)

## CONCLUSION

Evidence Room is not just a museum collection—it is a **regional knowledge hub** with the potential to shape how Tennessee's history is understood and taught for generations to come.

Strategic partnerships transform Evidence Room from an isolated resource into an **integrated part of the educational and cultural ecosystem** of East Tennessee and beyond. - Be realistic about resource constraints (small team, regional scope)
- Build authentic partnerships (not transactional)
- Generate measurable outcomes (so we know what's working)
- Scale sustainably (shift from building to sustaining in Year 2)
- Position Rocky Mount as a scholarly authority (not just a tourist attraction)

**Success** is not just traffic numbers—it's seeing high school students citing Evidence Room in a research paper, hearing a college professor say "this changed how I teach," and witnessing Cherokee Nation using our archive to tell their own community's story.

That's when we know the strategy is working.
