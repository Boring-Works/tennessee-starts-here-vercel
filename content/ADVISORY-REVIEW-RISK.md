### RISK #2A: CHEROKEE NATION CRITICISM ✅ SUBSTANTIALLY MITIGATED

**Original Assessment:** CRITICAL RISK (85% probability if no Cherokee voices)
**Current Status:** MEDIUM RISK (30-40% probability)

**Major Improvement:**
The Evidence Room has gone from **0% Cherokee-authored content to having TWO foundational Cherokee documents** that demonstrate Cherokee agency, sovereignty, and diplomatic sophistication.

**What Was Accomplished:**

**Document 1: Cherokee Delegation to Philadelphia (1792)**

- **Cultural Sensitivity:** ✅ EXCELLENT
- **Scholarly Quality:** ✅ VERIFIED (3 sources: Founders Online, American State Papers, Knox Papers)
- **Cherokee Agency:** ✅ STRONG - Shows Cherokee initiating diplomatic mission, forcing treaty amendments
- **Accuracy:** ✅ HIGH - Names five Cherokee leaders, documents their demands, shows successful negotiation
- **Connection to Rocky Mount:** ✅ CLEAR - Direct response to Treaty of Holston negotiated at Rocky Mount

**Key Strengths:**

- Document shows Cherokee as diplomatic agents, not passive subjects
- Demonstrates Cherokee success in forcing U.S. treaty modifications (annuity increase $1,000 → $1,500)
- Includes detail that Cherokee privately called Blount "the Dirt Captain" (shows critical analysis of American motivations)
- Translation verified by dual interpreters (James Carey and George Miller)

**Document 2: John Watts on Peace and Boundaries (1796)**

- **Cultural Sensitivity:** ✅ EXCELLENT
- **Scholarly Quality:** ✅ VERIFIED (Papers of War Department, pages 57-60)
- **Cherokee Agency:** ✅ EXCEPTIONAL - Shows Cherokee holding U.S. accountable five years after treaty
- **Technical Sophistication:** ✅ REMARKABLE - Watts demands surveyor "of integrity," specifies optimal survey season ("just as the Trees begin to put out their Buds"), requires Cherokee oversight
- **Sovereignty Assertion:** ✅ STRONG - "I would insist" language, demands accountability for unpunished murders of Cherokee

**Key Strengths:**

- Five years after Treaty of Holston, shows Cherokee tracking U.S. violations
- Demonstrates Cherokee technical knowledge of surveying practices
- Shows diplomatic sophistication in balancing firmness with protocol
- Documents U.S. failure to honor treaty terms (boundary never surveyed, encroachments continued)

**Timeline Integration:** ✅ WELL EXECUTED
Added 6 new timeline entries showing Cherokee diplomatic arc (1791-1796), properly linked to document IDs, marked as "featured" to highlight Cherokee perspective.

**Why Risk Remains MEDIUM (not LOW):**

While this is **substantial progress**, Cherokee Nation or academic critics might still note:

1. **Only 2 documents** - Cherokee representation is ~5-10% of collection (up from 0% but still minimal)
2. **No Cherokee Nation consultation yet** - Documents added without Cherokee Nation input or partnership
3. **All in English translation** - Pre-Sequoyah syllabary era, but critics might note translation limitations
4. **Missing key voices** - Little Turkey, Hanging Maw, other signatories still lack primary documents
5. **No "Cherokee Perspectives" section** - Documents exist but UI doesn't highlight them as distinct collection
6. **No "Missing Voices" acknowledgment** - Site doesn't explicitly acknowledge whose voices remain absent

**Recommended Next Actions to Reduce Risk to LOW:**

1. Add UI label for "Cherokee Perspectives" collection (2-3 hours)
2. Create "Missing Voices" acknowledgment page (3-4 hours)
3. Contact Cherokee Nation Cultural Resources AFTER adding 2-3 more documents (Phase 1 complete)

**Overall Assessment:** This is **excellent foundation work**. Cherokee Nation criticism risk has dropped from CRITICAL to MEDIUM. No longer a launch-blocking issue.

## PART 3: QUALITY OF CHEROKEE DOCUMENTS (CULTURAL SENSITIVITY REVIEW)

### Evaluation Criteria

As Risk Management Advisor, I assessed Cherokee documents against these standards:

1. **Agency vs. Objectification** - Do Cherokee appear as agents or objects?
2. **Accuracy of Representation** - Are names, roles, context correct?
3. **Cultural Sensitivity** - Any appropriative or colonial language?
4. **Scholarly Rigor** - Are sources verified, methods transparent?
5. **Connection to Rocky Mount** - Is relevance clearly explained?

### Cherokee Delegation to Philadelphia (1792) - ASSESSMENT

**Agency:** ✅ EXCELLENT
Cherokee portrayed as diplomatic agents who:

- Initiated contact with federal government (not summoned)
- Traveled hundreds of miles to present demands
- Successfully forced treaty modifications
- Demonstrated political analysis ("the Dirt Captain" nickname for Blount)

**Accuracy:** ✅ HIGH

- Five Cherokee leaders named with multiple name variations
- Six specific demands documented
- Translation verification method explained (dual interpreters)
- Outcome documented (annuity increase approved)
- Timeline shows arc of delegation visit (arrival, meetings, departure)

**Cultural Sensitivity:** ✅ GOOD with minor reservation
**Positive:**

- Document emphasizes Cherokee diplomatic sophistication
- Shows Cherokee forcing U.S. government to modify treaty terms
- Acknowledges translation limitations
- Respects Cherokee political strategies

**Minor Concern:**
Document is written ABOUT Cherokee (using Knox's report) rather than Cherokee's direct words. While historically accurate (Knox report is the available source), Cherokee voices are still mediated through white observers.

**Mitigation:** Document explicitly notes "Cherokee delegation's own speeches and statements were translated by James Carey and submitted as annexes to Knox's report." This acknowledges whose voice we're hearing.

**Scholarly Rigor:** ✅ EXCELLENT

- 3 verified sources (Founders Online, American State Papers, Knox Papers)
- Translation method documented
- Verification status: "verified"
- Source URLs provided
- Citation formats included

**Connection to Rocky Mount:** ✅ CLEAR
Document explains delegation protested treaty terms negotiated at Rocky Mount 6 months earlier. Shows Rocky Mount as one step in ongoing Cherokee diplomacy.

**Overall Grade:** **A- (Excellent with minor limitations acknowledged)**
