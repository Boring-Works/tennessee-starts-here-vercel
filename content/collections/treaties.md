## About This Collection

This collection contains the foundational legal documents of the Southwest Territory, including the Treaty of Holston and its additional article, Washington's proclamation making the treaty binding law, and the act establishing territorial government.

## Key Documents

- **Southwest Territory Act (1790):** Established the legal framework for territorial government
- **Treaty of Holston (1791):** Peace treaty with the Cherokee Nation, signed by 42 chiefs
- **Washington's Proclamation (1791):** Made the treaty binding federal law
- **Additional Article (1792):** Increased the annual payment to the Cherokee Nation

## Historical Significance

The Treaty of Holston represents a significant diplomatic achievement. Negotiated by Governor Blount at White's Fort, it established peace with the Cherokee Nation and defined boundaries that would shape Tennessee's development. The treaty also committed the United States to annual payments and trade guarantees, establishing a pattern for federal-tribal relations.
