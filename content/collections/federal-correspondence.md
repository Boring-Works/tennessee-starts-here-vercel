## About This Collection

This collection contains letters between the principal figures of the early federal government as they worked to establish American authority in the Southwest Territory. These documents reveal the policy considerations and personal relationships that shaped the frontier.

## Key Themes

- **The Governor Question:** Washington's deliberation over where the territorial capital should be located
- **Blount's Appointment:** The recommendation and confirmation process that made Blount governor
- **Treaty Authorization:** Instructions to Blount regarding negotiations with the Cherokee
- **Ratification Process:** The path from treaty signing to presidential proclamation

## Notable Correspondents

- **George Washington:** President, ultimately responsible for all territorial decisions
- **Henry Knox:** Secretary of War, oversaw Indian affairs and territorial governance
- **Thomas Jefferson:** Secretary of State, involved in treaty ratification
- **Hugh Williamson:** North Carolina congressman who recommended Blount
