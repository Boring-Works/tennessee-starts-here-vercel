## About This Collection

The Knoxville Gazette was the first newspaper published in the territory that would become Tennessee, and one of the first west of the Appalachians. Established by printer George Roulstone just weeks after the capital moved to Knoxville, it served as the primary source of news for the frontier community.

## Content Types

- **Official Proclamations:** Government announcements and legal notices
- **Foreign and Domestic News:** Reports from Philadelphia and beyond
- **Local Intelligence:** News from the Cherokee country and frontier settlements
- **Advertisements:** Commercial notices revealing the economic life of the territory
- **Prices Current:** Market prices for agricultural commodities

## Historical Significance

The Gazette documents the transformation of the Southwest Territory from a frontier region to a state. Its pages record the Treaty of Holston proclamation, the growth of Knoxville, the statehood movement, and finally the admission of Tennessee to the Union in 1796.
