## About This Collection

The Blount Papers comprise Governor William Blount's official correspondence with the War Department during his tenure as Governor of the Southwest Territory (1790-1796). These letters document the day-to-day challenges of establishing federal government on the frontier.

## Key Themes

- **Establishment of Government:** The practical challenges of organizing courts, militia, and civil administration
- **Indian Relations:** Blount's negotiations with Cherokee leaders leading to the Treaty of Holston
- **Frontier Security:** Managing tensions between settlers and Native Americans
- **Capital Development:** The transition from Rocky Mount to Knoxville

## Historical Significance

As the first and only Governor of the Southwest Territory, Blount's correspondence provides an unparalleled window into the founding era. His letters reveal both the grand vision of American expansion and the mundane realities of frontier governance.
