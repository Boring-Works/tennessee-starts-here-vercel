## Three Critical Problems

### 1. NO PRIMARY SOURCES EXIST

- No Jackson letters mentioning Rocky Mount
- No Cobb family receipts or records
- No contemporary 1788 accounts
- No diary entries
- Source: All evidence is secondary (written 100+ years later)

**Status:** Complete absence of primary documentation

### 2. BUILDING ANACHRONISM (Physical Impossibility)

- **Dendrochronology proves:** Current Rocky Mount structures built 1826-1830
- **Jackson's claimed visit:** 1788
- **Gap:** 38-42 YEARS before buildings existed
- **Built by:** Michael Massengill (Cobb's grandson), not original Cobbs

**Status:** Jackson could NOT have stayed in buildings visitors see today

### 3. GEOGRAPHIC INEFFICIENCY

- Jackson needed to reach Jonesborough (where his court license was)
- Rocky Mount is 30 miles PAST Jonesborough (backtracking)
- Direct route via Wilderness Road = more efficient
- Detour adds 130+ miles and 8-12 extra travel days

**Status:** No documented reason for Rocky Mount detour

---

## The Stronger Claim: Christopher Taylor House, Jonesborough

**Key Fact:** The Rocky Mount document itself states:

> "Christopher Taylor house in Jonesborough has stronger documented evidence of Jackson's lodging."

**Why This Matters:**

- Jackson was definitely in Jonesborough (August 1788, duel incident — documented)
- Jonesborough is where his business was (court license processing)
- Christopher Taylor house would be the logical lodging place
- Evidence Room acknowledges this claim is STRONGER than Rocky Mount

**Missing:** No separate Evidence Room document for Christopher Taylor house (should be added)

---

## Timeline Gap Problem

**Claimed timeline:**

- Spring 1788: Six weeks at Rocky Mount
- August 1788: Duel in Jonesborough (documented)
- October 1788: Nashville (documented)

**2-3 month gap:** June-July 1788 completely undocumented

**Logical issue:** If Jackson left Rocky Mount by early June, what was he doing June-July? Why isn't he documented in Jonesborough until August?

## What This Means for Visitors

**Current Reality:**

- Visitors tour Rocky Mount buildings
- Museum suggests Jackson stayed here in 1788
- **But these buildings were built 1826-1830**
- Visitors believe they're seeing where Jackson stayed (false)

**Accurate Statement Would Be:**

> "Jackson may have visited this property in 1788, according to historical tradition. However, the buildings you see were constructed in the 1820s-1830s, nearly 40 years later. If Jackson visited, it would have been in earlier structures that no longer exist."

## Bottom Line

### The Claim

"Andrew Jackson lodged at Rocky Mount for six weeks in Spring 1788"

### The Verdict

**UNVERIFIED TRADITION** — widely repeated in secondary sources, but completely lacking in primary documentation. Current buildings are too new to support the claim. Stronger evidence points to Jonesborough (Christopher Taylor house).

### The Evidence Standard

- **For:** Tennessee Encyclopedia, museum materials (100+ years of tradition)
- **Against:** Dendrochronology, zero primary sources, geographic inefficiency
- **Better claim:** Christopher Taylor house, Jonesborough (acknowledged as stronger by Evidence Room)

### What Visitors Should Know

Jackson definitely traveled through this region in 1788. He was documented in Jonesborough (August duel) and Nashville (October). Rocky Mount tradition is plausible but unproven. Buildings shown were constructed decades after his claimed visit.
