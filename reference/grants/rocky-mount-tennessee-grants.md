### WHY ROCKY MOUNT IS A PERFECT FIT

Rocky Mount represents one of the most significant founding-era sites in Tennessee and is directly aligned with the America 250 mission:

1. **First Territorial Capital:** Rocky Mount served as the de facto capital of the Southwest Territory from October 1790 to early 1792 - the very government structure that led directly to Tennessee statehood in 1796.

2. **Connection to Founding Fathers:** Governor William Blount, a signer of the U.S. Constitution, personally appointed by President George Washington, conducted territorial government business from Rocky Mount.

3. **Constitutional Governance Foundation:** Blount announced and implemented the Ordinance of 1787 and North Carolina's Act of Cession from Rocky Mount, establishing the legal framework for westward expansion.

4. **Census and Sovereignty:** The 1791 territorial enumeration (35,691 residents) was conducted from Rocky Mount, establishing the population records that would justify statehood.

5. **Revolutionary Era Timeline:** The site's interpretive period (1791) falls squarely within the grant's focus on "Revolutionary era to the present."

6. **Living History Museum:** As an operational living history museum since 1962, Rocky Mount actively interprets Tennessee's unique founding story to thousands of visitors annually.

---

### WHAT TO HIGHLIGHT IN APPLICATION

**Primary Talking Points:**

1. **"Tennessee's governmental story began here"** - Rocky Mount is where Tennessee's path to statehood was administered. When William Blount arrived on October 23, 1790, he transformed this private home into the seat of territorial government.

2. **"Direct connection to George Washington and the Constitution"** - Blount was Washington's personal appointment and a Constitutional Convention delegate. His work at Rocky Mount implemented the new Constitution in the western territories.

3. **"Foundation of Tennessee law and governance"** - From Rocky Mount, Blount re-commissioned all county officials, voiding North Carolina appointments and establishing new territorial governance for Washington, Sullivan, Greene, Hawkins, Davidson, Sumner, and Tennessee counties.

4. **"Native American diplomacy center"** - Rocky Mount hosted meetings that led to treaties with Cherokee and other nations, shaping Tennessee's boundaries.

5. **"The 60+ year partnership with the Tennessee Historical Commission"** - Demonstrates sustained commitment to preservation since 1962.

6. **"Living interpretation of 1791"** - The site recreates daily life during Blount's residency, making the founding era accessible to modern audiences.

## 3. TENNESSEE HISTORICAL COMMISSION HISTORIC PRESERVATION FUND GRANT

### Grant Name & Source

**Official Name:** Historic Preservation Fund Grant (HPF)
**Administering Agency:** Tennessee Historical Commission
**Funding Source:** Federal (oil and gas lease revenues, not tax dollars)
**Authorizing Legislation:** National Historic Preservation Act of 1966

### Award Amount

- **Typical Range:** $15,000 - $65,000
- **Federal Share:** Up to 60% of approved project costs
- **Required Match:** 40% from grantee
- **Pool:** Based on annual federal allocation to Tennessee

### Deadline

- **Application Period:** December 1, 2025 - February 1, 2026
- **Note:** Subject to federal budget approval
- **Review Period:** Following close, THC grant review team scores applications
- **Final Approval:** Top-scoring projects submitted to National Park Service

### Eligibility

**Property Requirements:**

- Must be listed on the National Register of Historic Places
- Must demonstrate public benefit (museums score highest)
- Properties must have demonstrated public use

**Eligible Projects:**

- Architectural and archaeological surveys
- National Register nominations
- Local architectural surveys
- Historic preservation design guidelines
- Restoration/rehabilitation of National Register-listed properties

### Application URL

**Apply Here:** TDEC Grant Management System (GMS)
**Instructions:** https://www.tn.gov/environment/about-tdec/grants/thc.html

### Contact Information

- **Federal Programs:** Holly Barnett
- **Email:** holly.m.barnett@tn.gov
- **Agency:** Tennessee Historical Commission
- **Address:** 2941 Lebanon Pike, Nashville, TN
- **Website:** https://www.tn.gov/historicalcommission/federal-programs/preservation-grants.html

---

### WHY ROCKY MOUNT IS A PERFECT FIT

Rocky Mount is an ideal candidate for HPF funding based on the program's scoring criteria:

1. **National Register Listed:** Rocky Mount is listed on the National Register of Historic Places, meeting the fundamental eligibility requirement.

2. **Maximum Public Benefit:** The grant program explicitly states that "properties will score highest in the grant ranking process that have a demonstrated public benefit. Examples include museums." Rocky Mount IS a museum with thousands of annual visitors.

3. **State Partnership:** Rocky Mount's partnership with the Tennessee Historical Commission (the granting agency) since 1962 demonstrates alignment with state preservation goals.

4. **Historic Significance:** As Tennessee's first territorial capital, Rocky Mount represents the highest tier of historical significance - foundational to Tennessee statehood.

5. **Restoration Priority:** Any preservation work directly contributes to interpreting the founding era - exactly what the National Historic Preservation Act was designed to support.

### WHAT TO HIGHLIGHT IN APPLICATION

4. **"Educational mission"** - Document school programs, visitor statistics, and community engagement.

First Horizon Foundation's Arts & Culture focus aligns well with Rocky Mount:

3. **Regional Commitment:** First Horizon has deep roots in Tennessee (formerly First Tennessee Bank) with branches throughout the region.

4. **Track Record:** The Foundation has funded major cultural institutions including Memphis Brooks Museum of Art, Nashville Public Radio, and ArtsBuild - demonstrating arts sector commitment.

5. **Local Banking Relationship:** If Rocky Mount or association members bank with First Horizon, this strengthens the relationship.

6. **Community Focus:** Private foundations often prioritize organizations with demonstrated community impact - Rocky Mount's 60-year history qualifies.

### APPLICATION TIPS

1. **Check deadline immediately** - Confirm January 31, 2026 deadline at firsthorizonfoundation.com.

2. **Banking relationship matters** - If Rocky Mount or board members bank with First Horizon, mention this. If not, consider opening an account.

3. **Board connections** - Research whether any First Horizon employees serve on Rocky Mount's board, or vice versa.

4. **Apply under correct region** - Select "East Tennessee" when applying, specifically mentioning Sullivan County.

5. **Be realistic about amount** - First-time applicants should request modest amounts ($5,000-$15,000) to build relationship.

6. **Connect to their priorities:**
   - Arts and Culture (primary fit)
   - Education & Leadership (school programs)
   - Environmental Sustainability (historic landscape)

7. **Demonstrate sustainability** - Private foundations want to know their investment has lasting impact.

8. **Strong financials required** - Have audited financials and 990s ready.

9. **Community impact metrics** - Quantify visitors, students served, economic impact.

10. **Follow up appropriately** - Build relationship with foundation staff for future applications.

11. **Consider multi-year request** - Foundations sometimes prefer predictable multi-year commitments.

12. **Avoid restricted topics** - Don't frame requests around travel, individual support, or religious themes.
