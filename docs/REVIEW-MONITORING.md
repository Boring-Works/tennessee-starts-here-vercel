# Review Monitoring & Response Process

Rocky Mount State Historic Site's comprehensive guide for monitoring, managing, and responding to online reviews across all platforms.

## Overview

This document establishes the standard operating procedures for:

- Monitoring reviews across platforms
- Responding to visitor feedback promptly
- Escalating issues appropriately
- Collecting quotes for marketing
- Maintaining our online reputation

## 1. Google Alert Setup Instructions

### Purpose

Automatically receive notifications when Rocky Mount is mentioned online. This ensures timely awareness of new reviews and mentions.

### Step-by-Step Setup

#### Create Google Alerts for Key Search Terms

1. **Go to Google Alerts**
   - Visit: https://www.google.com/alerts
   - Sign in with your Google account

2. **Create Alert #1: Rocky Mount State Historic Site**
   - Search term: `"Rocky Mount State Historic Site"`
   - Results type: Everything
   - How often: Daily
   - Deliver to: Email address

3. **Create Alert #2: Rocky Mount Museum Tennessee**
   - Search term: `"Rocky Mount Museum" Tennessee`
   - Results type: Everything
   - How often: Daily
   - Deliver to: Email address

4. **Create Alert #3: Rocky Mount Piney Flats**
   - Search term: `"Rocky Mount" "Piney Flats"`
   - Results type: Everything
   - How often: Daily
   - Deliver to: Email address

5. **Create Alert #4: Rocky Mount Tennessee Reviews**
   - Search term: `Rocky Mount Tennessee reviews`
   - Results type: News
   - How often: As-it-happens
   - Deliver to: Email address

#### Alert Configuration Best Practices

- **Frequency selection**: Daily digests reduce email fatigue while ensuring daily coverage
- **Multiple accounts**: Have alerts sent to both the main office email and director email
- **Archive alerts**: Keep all alerts in a dedicated folder for reference
- **Review sources**: Results will include TripAdvisor, Google Reviews, Facebook, and local blogs

## 3. Response SLA (Service Level Agreement)

All reviews require a response. Response times vary by review rating and content.

### Service Level Standards

| Rating     | Response Time   | Priority | Response Tone                          |
| ---------- | --------------- | -------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **5-star** | Within 48 hours | Standard | Warm, appreciative, personal           |
| **4-star** | Within 48 hours | Standard | Appreciative, gently address concern   |
| **3-star** | Within 24 hours | Medium   | Professional, solution-focused         |
| **2-star** | Within 24 hours | High     | Professional, empathetic, escalate     |
| **1-star** | Within 24 hours | Critical | Professional, director review required |

### Response Examples

#### 5-Star Response Template

```
Thank you so much for taking time to write this wonderful review!
We're thrilled you enjoyed your visit to Rocky Mount. Your mention
of [specific detail] really made our day. We'd love to see you again
soon!

Best regards,
[Name]
Rocky Mount State Historic Site
```

#### 4-Star Response Template

```
Thank you for this thoughtful review! We're so glad you enjoyed
[positive aspect]. We appreciate your feedback about [constructive comment].
This helps us continuously improve the visitor experience. We hope
to welcome you back soon!

Best regards,
[Name]
Rocky Mount State Historic Site
```

#### 3-Star Response Template

```
Thank you for taking the time to visit and share your feedback.
We appreciate your comments about [issue]. This is valuable input
for our team. We're committed to [specific improvement], and we'd
appreciate another chance to provide a better experience.

Please feel free to reach out directly if we can assist further:
[phone/email]
```

## 4. Escalation Process

### Negative Review Handling

#### Criteria for Escalation to Director

Escalate immediately (within 1 hour) to the Executive Director if:

- 1-star or 2-star reviews
- Review mentions safety concerns
- Review mentions inappropriate staff conduct
- Review contains false factual claims
- Review mentions health/sanitation issues
- Multiple negative reviews received in short timeframe
- Reviewer is known/local media/influencer

#### Escalation Procedure

1. **Capture evidence**
   - Screenshot full review
   - Document review URL
   - Record platform, date, reviewer name
   - Save to: `/shared/reviews/escalations/[date]/`

2. **Notify Director**
   - Email escalation summary with attachments
   - Include context: What complaint is about
   - Flag if response urgency is high
   - Mark in shared escalation log

3. **Director Review & Response**
   - Director will review within 2 hours
   - Director drafts response or directs staff response
   - All responses documented in escalation log

4. **Follow-up**
   - Document any outcome (reviewer responded, issue resolved, etc.)
   - Add to team discussion agenda if systemic issue identified

### When to Involve Legal/PR

**Legal involvement** (notify director for coordination):

- Claims of injury or liability
- Threats of litigation
- Demands for compensation
- Review mentioning law enforcement involvement

**PR involvement** (director decision):

- Review going viral (100+ shares/comments)
- Negative coverage by local media
- Coordinated negative review campaign
- Review affecting bookings/attendance

## 5. Quote Collection Guidelines

Strategic collection of visitor quotes for website, social media, and marketing materials.

### What Makes a Quote "Website-Worthy"

**Essential qualities:**

- Specific: Mentions particular experience or staff member
- Authentic: Genuine visitor voice (not generic praise)
- Positive impact: Highlights key visitor value proposition
- Quotable length: 1-2 sentences maximum
- No personal details: Doesn't identify specific visitors by last name
- Relevant to audiences: Speaks to families, history buffs, or tour groups

**Examples of Quote-Worthy Reviews:**

✓ "The docent's knowledge of the mountain settlement era was incredible. We learned more in two hours than we expected."

✓ "Our kids were engaged the entire tour—they actually asked to come back!"

✓ "A hidden gem. The views are spectacular and the house feels like stepping back in time."

✗ "Nice place" (too generic)

✗ "The [staff member's] tour on [date] was excellent" (too specific with personal identifier)

✗ "The gift shop is overpriced" (negative)

### Permission & Attribution Process

#### Requesting Permission

1. **For 5-star reviews in management portals:**
   - Email reviewer within 5 days of review
   - Use template below
   - Request response within 10 days

2. **For naturally shared visitor feedback:**
   - If verbal, document with date and visitor name
   - Follow up via email confirmation

#### Permission Request Email Template

```
Subject: May we share your kind words?

Hi [Visitor First Name],

Thank you so much for your wonderful review on [Platform]!
Your comment about [specific detail] was so meaningful to our team.

We'd love to share your feedback on our website to help other
visitors learn about the experience you had. May we use this quote:

"[EXACT QUOTE]"

And may we credit it as: "- [Visitor First Name], [State]"

Please reply to confirm—thank you!

Warmly,
Rocky Mount State Historic Site
```

### Attribution Format Standards

**On website:**

- "The views are spectacular." — **Jennifer, North Carolina**
- Short format: "Jennifer, North Carolina" only

- Attribution with state

### Executive Director

- Reviews and approves responses to negative/sensitive reviews
- Handles escalated issues and strategic PR decisions
- Reviews monthly sentiment report
- Approves quote usage for marketing

### Staff Member (Primary Monitor)

- Performs daily/weekly review monitoring (see Section 2 checklist)

## 10. Best Practices & Tips

### Do's

- ✓ Respond within SLA targets
- ✓ Personalize responses (mention specific detail from review)
- ✓ Use reviewer's first name
- ✓ Keep tone warm and genuine
- ✓ Include call to action ("We'd love to see you again")
- ✓ Thank reviewers, even if critique is harsh
- ✓ Stay professional at all times

### Don'ts

- ✗ Ignore negative reviews
- ✗ Respond defensively or argue with reviewers
- ✗ Use overly formal/robotic language
- ✗ Make excuses
- ✗ Publicly discuss other reviewers
- ✗ Share private information
- ✗ Miss SLA targets without reason

### Pro Tips

- **Screenshot as you go:** Use browser screenshot tool to capture reviews as you read them
- **Batch respond:** Set aside 30 minutes daily for review responses
- **Watch for patterns:** Track if multiple reviews mention same issue
- **Follow up after resolution:** If you resolve a visitor's complaint, consider reaching out to ask if they'd update their review
- **Train replacements:** Document any special situations or regular reviewers
