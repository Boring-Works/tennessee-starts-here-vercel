# Event Staff Implementation Guide

## Rocky Mount Synergy Program - Quick Reference

**For:** All event staff, check-in coordinators, tour guides, program directors
**Last Updated:** January 29, 2026
**Training Time Required:** 30 minutes

---

## WHAT YOU NEED TO KNOW (The 2-Minute Version)

### The Big Idea

Each event is a door into a larger story. Your job is to help visitors see the **next door**.

**The Pipeline:**

```
Visitor arrives → Experience the event → Learn what's next → Come back
```

**Your Role:** Make the "what's next" irresistible.

---

## AT CHECK-IN (Every Visitor)

### Step 1: Passport Introduction (15 seconds)

**If first-time visitor:**

"Welcome! We have a cool program. If you visit events throughout the season, you can collect stamps and earn rewards. Want to start your passport?"

**If returning visitor:**

"Great to see you again! Do you have your passport? Let's scan it!"

### Step 2: Distribute Passport (If Applicable)

- **Road to 250 & Spring events** → Tier 1 (Frontier Explorer, blue cover)
- **Lecture series & Early Frontier Days** → Tier 2 (Government Witness, tan cover)
- **September onwards** → Tier 3 (First Family, leather-bound)

**If visitor says "no thanks":**

- Respect it. Hand them a "Next Event" card instead.
- Don't push. Availability is persuasive.

### Step 3: Scan or Stamp

**Smartphone users (preferred):**

- Point phone at QR code on your booth
- Phone shows: "Great! [Event name]. You're [X] events toward [tier name]"
- Done.

**No smartphone:**

- Use stamp. Mark event name + date.
- Say: "You can email us a photo of your stamps, or just bring it next time."

### Step 4: Ask the Critical Question

"After this event, which sounds interesting to you—a lecture, a workshop, or a festival?"

**Listen.** This tells you what to recommend.

**Their answer → Your next action:**

| They Say              | You Recommend                     | Say This                                                             |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "Lectures sound cool" | Next lecture coming up            | "Dr. [Speaker] is coming [date]. Want a flyer?"                      |
| "I want hands-on"     | Workshops, camps, festivals       | "We have Blacksmith Workshops [dates]. Kids love them."              |
| "Just browsing"       | Twilight Tours, Behind the Scenes | "We have evening tours if day visits don't work. Free to check out." |

## THE FOUR CRITICAL SCRIPTS

### Script 1: "You're Standing Where Government Started"

**Use this at:** All events, especially Road to 250, Stitching Independence, July 4

**Who Says It:** Any staff member

**When:** Greeting visitors

**The Words:**
"Welcome to Rocky Mount. You're standing where Tennessee's government was born. In 1791, this ground was the capitol of the entire frontier. Today, we're celebrating the 250th birthday of the nation that started here. Everything you'll see today is part of that story."

**Why:** Orients visitors to the significance immediately. Makes them feel important.

---

### Script 2: "You Can't Get This Story at Home"

**Use this at:** Lectures, Behind the Scenes, festivals

**Who Says It:** Event coordinator or tour guide

**When:** Mid-event or when upselling tours

**The Words:**
"The textbooks say [brief, dry fact]. But here's what the textbooks miss: [specific local detail]. We have artifacts, primary sources, and interpreters who've spent years on this. If you want the deep story, come back for a tour."

**Example:**

- Textbook: "Mary Patton made gunpowder at Kings Mountain."
- Behind the scenes: "Mary Patton made 500 pounds by hand, using sulfur, charcoal, and saltpeter—and if the mixture was wrong, the whole supply exploded. We have her original powder horn here. She's not a name in a chapter. She's a woman who risked her life."

**Why:** Creates differentiation between "you were here" and "you understood."

---

### Script 3: "Tour Upsell" (If Someone's Interested)

**Use this at:** Any event, after engagement with Script 2

**Who Says It:** Any staff member

**When:** Visitor shows interest in history/artifacts

**The Words:**
"Our daily tours take you through the building, the grounds, and the artifact vault. You'll see things like [specific item]. Guides tell the stories behind each room. Tours run [time]. Want to add one to your visit today, or book for another day?"

**Offer options:**

- "We have a tour in 30 minutes if you have time now."
- "Or, book online for next week—your discount [if applicable]."
- "If you prefer evening tours, we have Twilight Tours [dates]."

**Why:** Removes friction. Gives choices. Makes it easy to say yes.

---

### Script 4: "The Roadmap to Your Reward"

**Use this at:** Checkout, after passport scan

**Who Says It:** Check-out/checkout staff

**When:** Visitor is leaving

**The Words:**

_If they're 1-2 events from completion:_
"You're just two events away from [reward]! If you come to [next two events], you'll unlock a $25 gift card / $50 gear / lifetime pass. That's pretty cool."

_If they're halfway through:_
"You're doing great. Keep going and you'll get [reward] by [date]. We'll email you progress updates so you remember where you are."

_If they're just started:_
"You're on the journey. Come back whenever you can. Every event adds to the story. No rush."

**Why:** Creates forward momentum. Makes the reward concrete and achievable.

## DAILY TOUR INTEGRATION AT EVENTS

### The Core Idea

Festivals ≠ Substitute for tours. Festivals + tours = complete experience.

### Your Role: Tour Ambassador

**What to Do:**

1. At your event, identify visitors who are _deeply engaged_ (asking questions, taking notes)
2. Say: "Would you like to learn more about [artifact/location]? Our guides can show you details most visitors miss."
3. Direct them to tour info/booking station

**What to Say:**

_For visual learners:_
"Come take a tour. You'll see how the rooms connect, and understand why William Cobb chose this exact location."

_For families with kids:_
"Tours have hands-on activities. Kids can help our interpreters do period tasks. Way more fun than just looking."

_For adults:_
"If you want the deep story—original documents, artifact collections, curator insights—tours are where that lives."

### Tour Booking Logistics

- **During event:** If tour capacity available, book on-site (5 min slots)
- **Post-event:** Hand out "Tour Info" card with website + phone number
- **Next-day follow-up:** Staff calls/emails visitors with tour times

### Concurrent Program Cross-Promotion

**If event attendee asks "what else can we do?"**

Offer in priority order:

1. **Twilight Tours** (summer Thursdays): "Evening tours when it's cooler. Same great guide, fewer crowds."
2. **Behind the Scenes** (monthly): "Super exclusive. Handle 200-year-old artifacts. 10 people only."
3. **Blacksmith Workshop** (select Saturdays): "Make something you take home. Best one-on-one experience."

## TROUBLESHOOTING: COMMON SITUATIONS

### Situation: Visitor Complains About Cost

**Your Response:**
"I hear you. Events are $15-35 per person. If cost is a barrier, we have free events [list: Road to 250, Sullivan County 250, OVTA Victory Day]. Plus, if you do 5 events, the $25 gift card helps offset costs."

**Escalation:**
If visitor still hesitant, get manager. Manager can discuss family passes or discounts for repeat visitors.

---

### Situation: Visitor Says "I Already Got This Story From Google"

**Your Response:**
"That's true! You can read the facts online. But you can't experience standing in the room where it happened, handling artifacts William Cobb touched, talking to our interpreters who've spent years with these stories. That's what we offer that the internet can't."

---

### Situation: Visitor Says "I Won't Have Time to Do All Events"

**Your Response:**
"Neither will most people! Tier 1 is just 5 events—you pick whichever appeal to you. No pressure. Even if you just come to two or three, you're part of something bigger. That's the point."

---

### Situation: Visitor Asks "Can I Transfer My Passport?"

**Your Response:**
"No, passports are personal. But your family member can start their own! You'll both earn rewards. Or, if you just want to give them a gift—we have gift cards."

---

### Situation: Visitor Complains Passport QR Code Didn't Work

**Your Response:**
"No problem! Happens sometimes. Let me stamp it for you right now. [Stamp]. And I've got your email. We can manually add this to your record."

## KEY METRICS YOU'RE HELPING TRACK

Every time you scan or stamp, you're collecting data that helps us understand:

- Which events bring new vs. returning visitors
- Which next-event recommendations work best
- Which passport tier people are most interested in
- How many people tour after attending events

**This helps us improve next year.** Your work matters.

## ONE FINAL REMINDER

Your job isn't to pressure anyone. It's to **show them the door**.

If someone says "no thanks" to a passport, a tour, a lecture—that's fine. Respect their choice. The best marketing is a visitor who had such a great time they come back on their own.

But **if someone asks "what's next?"—that's your moment.** Give them a clear, enthusiastic answer. Make the next step irresistible.
