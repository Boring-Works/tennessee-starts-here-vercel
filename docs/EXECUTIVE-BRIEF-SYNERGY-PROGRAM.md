# Executive Brief: Event Synergy Program

## Rocky Mount State Historic Site - America 250 2026

**For:** Executive Director, Board of Directors, Stakeholders
**Length:** 2-3 minute read
**Date:** January 29, 2026

---

## THE OPPORTUNITY

Rocky Mount has **26 events in 2026**. Historically, they operate as independent experiences—visitors attend one or two, then leave.

**The Problem:**

- No narrative arc connecting events
- Visitors don't understand why each event matters in relation to others
- No incentive to return after first visit
- Repeat visitor rate is 30-40% (lower than peer sites)
- Tour attendance static despite 26 event opportunities

**The Win:**
We can transform Rocky Mount from "a place you visit" into "a place you return to throughout the season." Each event becomes a door to the next experience. Visitors see themselves as part of a season-long story about Tennessee's founding.

---

## THE STRATEGY (In One Picture)

```
SPRING: "WHY?"            SUMMER: "THIS IS IT!"      FALL: "WHO WAS HERE?"
Context Building          Peak Experience             Genealogy Pivot
↓                        ↓                            ↓
Road to 250 →            Father's Day Forge →        Cherokee Heritage →
Camps + Lectures →       July 4 CEREMONY ★           First Families Reunion →
Early Frontier Days      (emotional climax)          OVTA Victory Day
↓                        ↓                            ↓
                    AUTUMN: "REMEMBER"              WINTER: "CELEBRATE"
                    Seasonal Tradition              Gift-Giving Closure
                    ↓                                ↓
                    Harvest Fest →                  Frontier Christmas →
                    Haunting →                      Candlelight Christmas
                    (tradition activation)           (sells out every year)
```

**The Mechanism:** Every event points visitors toward the next event. Marketing staff emphasize continuity. Rewards incentivize multi-event attendance. Tours deepen single events into deeper education.

**Result:** Visitors go from 1-2 events → 5-12 events per season. July 4 becomes unmissable (First 250 ceremony). Candlelight Christmas books months in advance.

---

## THE THREE PASSPORT TIERS

Transform isolated attendance into a **ladder of engagement:**

### Tier 1: Frontier Explorer

- **For:** Families (primary target)
- **Required:** Attend 5 events (choose from 7)
- **Reward:** $25 gift card
- **Expected Adoption:** 50-60% of visitors
- **Expected Completion:** 40-50% of adopters
- **ROI:** Low cost, high volume

### Tier 2: Government Witness

- **For:** Educators, history enthusiasts
- **Required:** Attend all 5 lectures + 3 major festivals
- **Reward:** $50 gear + VIP Behind the Scenes experiences (lifetime benefit)
- **Expected Adoption:** 20-30% of visitors
- **Expected Completion:** 20-25% of adopters
- **ROI:** Moderate cost, high loyalty

### Tier 3: First Family

- **For:** Genealogy researchers, deep-history travelers
- **Required:** Attend all 10 major events throughout season
- **Reward:** Engraved medal, leather journal, lifetime pass (5 years), name on permanent plaque
- **Expected Adoption:** 80-100 applications
- **Expected Completion:** 30-40 (30-40%)
- **ROI:** High cost per person, highest lifetime value

## THE FINANCIAL CASE

### Investment: $10,750

- Passport printing: $600
- Rewards (all tiers): $7,750
- QR code infrastructure: $200
- Staff training: $300
- Email/digital infrastructure: $400
- Fulfillment labor: $1,000

### Return on Investment

**Conservative Estimate:**

- 200+ Tier 1 completers × $25 = $5,000 in rewards
- 40 Tier 2 completers × $50 = $2,000 in rewards
- 35 Tier 3 completers × $100 = $3,500 in rewards (lifetime passes cost)
- **Total reward cost: ~$10,500**

**Revenue Generated:**

- Additional July 4 attendance: 100-150 people @ $25 avg = $2,500-3,750
- Additional tour bookings: +30% driven by event integration = ~$3,000 incremental
- Additional camp enrollment (family adoption lift): ~$5,000
- Gift shop sales (passport rewards + repeat visits): ~$2,000
- **Total estimated revenue: $12,500-13,750**

**Payback:** 1:1.2 to 1:1.3x in first year + recurring value in year 2+

**Three-Year Total ROI:** ~3:1 (accounting for multi-year passports and retained customers)

## CRITICAL SUCCESS FACTORS

**This program only works if:**

1. **Every staff member can explain it** (30-min training)
2. **QR codes + stamps work flawlessly** (tested daily)
3. **Passports are distributed at 50%+ of events** (critical mass)
4. **Email reminders go out monthly** ("You're 2 events away from reward")
5. **Tour integration happens at every event** (not afterthought)
6. **Rewards are fulfilled within 2 weeks** (maintain momentum)

**If any of these fail, attendance growth will plateau.**

---

## THE COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE

### What We're Building

A **"loyalty ladder"** that competitors can't easily replicate:

- Multi-tier system rewards different visitor types
- Lifetime passes create recurring revenue
- Plaque naming creates emotional investment
- Email list becomes marketing asset
- Data shows which events drive which visitors (optimization opportunity)

### What Makes This Unique

Unlike generic "visit all state parks" loyalty programs, this is **narrative-based**:

- Visitors aren't collecting random stamps
- Each event answers a specific question about Tennessee's founding
- The journey has emotional arcs (spring learning → summer peak → fall genealogy → winter celebration)
- Completion feels like becoming part of the story, not just collecting badges

## RISKS & MITIGATION

| Risk                          | Impact                   | Mitigation                                             |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Low passport adoption**     | Program dies at scale    | Train staff relentlessly; make passports irresistible  |
| **QR code failures**          | Frustration, abandonment | Backup stamp system; test daily                        |
| **Reward fulfillment delays** | Momentum loss            | 2-week fulfillment SLA; pre-order rewards              |
| **Tier 3 becomes elitist**    | Community backlash       | Celebrate all tiers equally; no gatekeeping            |
| **Budget overruns**           | Financial stress         | Cap Tier 2 gear (50 max), Tier 3 (40 max)              |
| **Staff resistance**          | Weak implementation      | Incentivize staff; show them it makes their job easier |
