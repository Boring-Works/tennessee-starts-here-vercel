## The Elevator Pitch

> "Rocky Mount is where Tennessee's government began. The ground is the artifact. The house is the proof. Stand where they stood."

---

## The Core Insight

**The ground is inarguable.** Dendrochronology can date a building. Nobody can dispute that the land is the same land. The dirt Blount walked on is the dirt visitors walk on.

### The Through-Line

> "The buildings evolved. The ground endures. Tennessee started here."

---

## The Problem & Solution

### The Problem

1. Dendrochronology proves the main house dates to **1827-1830**, not 1770
2. "First Western Capital" claims are challenged by Marietta, Ohio (1788)
3. We are often confused with Sycamore Shoals (military muster) or Exchange Place (frontier lifestyle)

### The Solution

We shift the "artifact status" from the logs to the land. This is **The Gettysburg Principle**: Nobody asks what year the Gettysburg Visitor Center was built; they come to stand on the ground.

### The Blue Ocean Lane

> Sycamore Shoals owns the muster. Exchange Place owns the farm. **Rocky Mount owns the statehouse.**
>
> They provided the muscle; we provided the mind. We are the other half of the origin story.

## Plan B: The Authority (22/25)

**Best For:** Grants, Press, Academic, Signage, Permanent Identity

### Primary Identity

> "Where Tennessee's Government Began"

### The Marietta Defense

When precision matters—especially for America 250 federal grants—use this exact phrasing:

> "The first federal seat of government established under the U.S. Constitution west of the Appalachian Mountains."

**Why this works:** Marietta, Ohio (1788) operated under the Articles of Confederation. Rocky Mount (1790) was established after the Constitution was ratified, making it the first Constitutional federal seat west of the mountains. This is technically accurate and defensible.

### The Sycamore Separation

### The Building Pivot

- **Blount** gives us Authority (Constitutional signer, Governor)

## Staff Scripts & Training

### Welcome Script (Arrival)

> "Welcome to Rocky Mount. Before there was a Tennessee, there was this ground. The Cobbs settled here in 1770—six years before the Declaration of Independence. In 1780, they supplied the Overmountain Men who turned the tide at Kings Mountain. And in 1790, Governor William Blount made these grounds the capital of the Southwest Territory. For approximately 16 months, this was the seat of power for everything that would become Tennessee. The buildings have evolved. The ground you're standing on is the constant. You are standing where they stood."

### FAQ: Building Age Question

**When a visitor asks:** "Is this the original building from 1770?"

> "Great question. The Cobb House was built in the 1820s by Michael Massengill, William Cobb's grandson. And that's actually the best part of the story—it proves the Cobb family didn't just survive on this frontier. They thrived. Three generations on the exact same soil. The buildings evolved. The ground endures. When Governor Blount governed from here in 1790, he stood on this same ground you're standing on right now."

### Sycamore Shoals Differentiation

**When a visitor asks:** "Is this where the soldiers gathered?"

> "No, the soldiers gathered at Sycamore Shoals—that was the muster point. Rocky Mount was the operational headquarters. This is where the family lived who supplied the gunpowder and horses. And later, this is where the government was actually formed. The Army gathered at the Shoals. The State started here."

### Tone Guidance: Friendly Rivalry

When differentiating from Sycamore Shoals, use a **clarifying tone, not dismissive**.

> "They provided the muscle; we provided the mind. We are the other half of the origin story—complementary, not competing."

### The Blount Credibility

Use when emphasizing Constitutional authority:

> "Governor Blount wasn't just any federal appointee—he was one of the 39 men who signed the U.S. Constitution in Philadelphia. Washington sent him here to govern the frontier. From this ground, he corresponded directly with President Washington and conducted the federal business that would become Tennessee."

### The Jackson Connection

When visitors ask about famous residents:

> "Andrew Jackson lodged here for six weeks in 1788 while waiting for his license to practice law in nearby Jonesborough. He was a young man then—decades before he became the 7th President. Governor Blount governed here. Andrew Jackson lodged here. The State started here."

| **Sep 18, 1790** | Blount visits President Washington at Mount Vernon                 |

| **Oct 11, 1790** | Blount arrives at William Cobb's home, Rocky Mount                 |
| **Oct 20, 1790** | Blount writes "Glass Windows" letter describing Rocky Mount        |
| **Oct 23, 1790** | Blount proclaims territorial government operational at Rocky Mount |

| Date             | Event                                                             |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **1791**         | Census records 35,691 residents; 7 counties across 2 districts    |
