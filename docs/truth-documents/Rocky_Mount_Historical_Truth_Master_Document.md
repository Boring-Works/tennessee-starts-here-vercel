# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Rocky Mount State Historic Site served as the first capital of the Southwest Territory (1790-1792), where Governor William Blount established the governmental machinery that led to Tennessee's statehood in 1796. The site represents Tennessee's oldest documented farm (established 1775 per TN Century Farms Program) and stands on land settled by William Cobb around 1770. This document corrects common historical misstatements and provides verified facts for all public communications.

# PART 3: CHRONOLOGICAL TIMELINE (VERIFIED)

## 1769-1770: Settlement Era

- William Cobb arrives and settles the land
- Pre-dates American independence (Declaration signed 1776)

## 1775: Farm Establishment

- Farm documented/established (per TN Century Farms Program)
- Makes this Tennessee's oldest documented agricultural operation

## 1780: Revolutionary War Era

- Cobb family supplies Overmountain Men for Kings Mountain campaign
- Direct connection to Revolutionary War victory

## 1790: Southwest Territory Created

- Congress establishes Southwest Territory (south of Ohio River)
- President George Washington appoints William Blount as territorial governor
- **Location of appointment: Philadelphia** (Washington never visited Rocky Mount)

## 1790-1792: Rocky Mount as Territorial Capital

- Governor Blount administers territorial government from Rocky Mount
- First federal government presence south of the Appalachian Mountains
- Governmental machinery laid groundwork for Tennessee statehood

## 1796: Tennessee Statehood

- Tennessee becomes 16th state (first admitted after original 13)
- State capital established in Knoxville (not Rocky Mount)

## 1820s: Current Building Construction

- Cobb-Massengill House built (the structure visitors see today)
- Confirmed by University of Tennessee dendrochronology study
- Building post-dates territorial capital period

## 2021-2022: Modern Era

- Rocky Mount Historical Association purchases 15 acres of historic farmland
- $365K capital campaign
- Expands site to include original farm acreage

# PART 5: SULLIVAN COUNTY vs. WASHINGTON COUNTY CLAIMS

## The "Where Tennessee Began" Argument

### Washington County's Legitimate Claims:

- **Oldest county in Tennessee** (established 1777)
- **Jonesborough = oldest town** (chartered 1779)
- **First in chronological terms**

### Sullivan County's Legitimate Claims:

- **Where Tennessee's government operated** (Rocky Mount = territorial capital 1790-1792)
- **Where statehood machinery functioned**
- **Where the territory-to-state transition occurred**

## The Distinction That Matters

**"Oldest" vs. "Where Government Began"**

- Washington County: Oldest **chronologically** (first county, first town)
- Sullivan County: Where **governmental functions** occurred (territorial capital, path to statehood)

**Both claims are accurate but describe different things:**

- Washington: "Tennessee's oldest county and town"
- Sullivan: "Where Tennessee began as a government / Where Tennessee's government began"

- The building post-dates Blount's time

# PART 11: COMMON QUESTIONS & APPROVED ANSWERS

## "Was Rocky Mount the first territorial capital?"

**Answer:** Rocky Mount was the first capital of the Southwest Territory (1790-1792), the territory south of the Ohio River that became Tennessee. Marietta, Ohio served as the capital of the Northwest Territory starting in 1788, two years before Rocky Mount.

## "Did George Washington visit Rocky Mount?"

**Answer:** There is no documented evidence of George Washington visiting Rocky Mount. Washington appointed William Blount as territorial governor in Philadelphia, and Blount established his headquarters at Rocky Mount.

## "How old is the building?"

**Answer:** The Cobb-Massengill House that visitors see today was built in the 1820s, confirmed by University of Tennessee dendrochronology studies. The site itself was settled around 1770 and served as the territorial capital from 1790-1792.

## "Is this Tennessee's oldest farm?"

**Answer:** Rocky Mount is documented by the Tennessee Century Farms Program as Tennessee's oldest documented farm, established in 1775.

## "Why does Sullivan County claim 'Where Tennessee Began' when Washington County is older?"

**Answer:** Both counties have legitimate but different claims. Washington County (1777) and Jonesborough (1779) are Tennessee's oldest county and town chronologically. Rocky Mount in Sullivan County is where Tennessee's government operated during the territorial period (1790-1792), making it where Tennessee began as a governmental entity on the path to statehood.
