### Six Review Criteria (What Reviewers Score)

**1. Significance (25 points maximum)**

- National/regional significance of materials
- Impact on scholarship and public understanding
- Contribution to 2026 Semiquincentennial
- **Rocky Mount strength:** First Southwest Territory capital, Revolutionary War connections

**2. Quality & Significance of Collections (25 points)**

- Scope and depth of materials
- Uniqueness or rarity
- Research value
- Current accessibility limitations
- **Rocky Mount strength:** Unprocessed territorial-era documents, material culture from founding period

**3. Methodology & Work Plan (20 points)**

- Clear project activities
- Realistic timeline
- Appropriate professional standards (DACS, Dublin Core, FADGI)
- Risk mitigation strategies
- **Rocky Mount strength:** Partnership with ETSU, professional archivist hire, established standards

**4. Project Staff & Organizational Capacity (15 points)**

- Qualifications of personnel
- Track record of completing projects
- Organizational stability
- Facilities and equipment
- **Rocky Mount strength:** THC partnership, capital campaign success, board stability

**5. Sustainability (10 points)**

- Long-term preservation plan
- Ongoing access after grant ends
- Maintenance commitments
- Training for future staff
- **Rocky Mount strength:** THC backing, endowment potential, PastPerfect Online subscription

**6. Budget & Cost-Effectiveness (5 points)**

- Clear alignment between budget and activities
- Reasonable costs
- Cost-sharing if any
- Efficient use of funds
- **Rocky Mount strength:** Itemized budget, vendor quotes, no indirect cost markup

## PART 2: TN250 PROJECT SUPPORT GRANT

### Complete Application - Exhibitions & Programs Category

**Amount Requested:** $20,000 (maximum allowed per category)  
**Deadline:** January 20, 2026, 11:59 PM CST (Round 2)  
**Status:** Submitted

### Project Summary (2,780 characters)

Rocky Mount State Historic Site served as the first seat of government for the Southwest Territory from 1790-1792, laying key groundwork for Tennessee statehood. As Tennessee's oldest documented farm (est. 1775 per Century Farms), Rocky Mount has developed entirely new programming for America 250. This grant funds six new events and our first-ever lecture series created specifically for the Semiquincentennial. This is not enhancement of existing programs—this is new programming interpreting Tennessee's founding story for the 250th anniversary of American independence.

**PROJECT TYPE:** Exhibitions and Programs - festivals, performances, educational lectures, and community history projects.

**NEW PROGRAMS CREATED FOR AMERICA 250:**

1. **Road to 250 Season Opening** (March 4) - Launch event marking the start of Rocky Mount's commemorative year, featuring unveiling of America 250 programming and launch of "First 250" community enrollment initiative.

2. **America 250 Lecture Series** - Five scholarly presentations representing Rocky Mount's first-ever lecture series:
   - Dr. James P. Byrd, Vanderbilt University: Colonial Religion on the Frontier
   - Lisa Bennett as Mary Patton: Powder for Kings Mountain
   - Thomas Bachelor: The Overmountain Men
   - Dr. Whitfield: William Blount and Tennessee Government
   - Cherokee & Settlers: Frontier Diplomacy

3. **Early Frontier Days & Colonial Trade Fair** (May 22-24) - Three-day festival interpreting Revolutionary-era trade, craftsmanship, and daily frontier life.

4. **America 250 Flag Weekend** (June 13-14) - Tennessee's 230th anniversary recognition, flag dedication ceremony, and "Betsy Ross Sip & Sew" program.

5. **Colonial Independence Day** (July 4) - Signature event celebrating America's 250th birthday at the site where Governor William Blount administered the Southwest Territory, featuring "First 250" ceremony reading enrolled community member names.

6. **First Families of Tennessee Reunion** (September 11-13) - Brand new annual event bringing together descendants of Tennessee's founding families.

**Enhanced programming:** Woolly Days (April 25-26) and Harvest Fest (October 10-11) receive America 250 components.

**Goals:** Launch 6 new public events; deliver Rocky Mount's first-ever 5-part lecture series; enroll 250 community members in First 250 commemorative program; reach 5,000+ attendees across all programs.

**Audience:** Families with children, K-12 students and educators, history enthusiasts, descendants of Tennessee founding families, Sullivan County residents, and heritage tourists.
