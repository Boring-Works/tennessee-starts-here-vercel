**Purpose:** Comprehensive strategy for positioning Sullivan County as "Where Tennessee Began" for America 250 and beyond, with verified historical claims and competitive positioning.

**Last Updated:** January 31, 2026

---

# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Sullivan County possesses the strongest historical claim to "Where Tennessee Began" based on Rocky Mount's role as the first capital of the Southwest Territory (1790-1792), where Governor William Blount established the governmental machinery that led to Tennessee's statehood in 1796. While Washington County can legitimately claim to be Tennessee's oldest county (1777) and Jonesborough its oldest town (1779), Sullivan County is where Tennessee began as a governmental entity. This distinction provides the foundation for a compelling heritage tourism narrative centered on America's 250th anniversary.

---

# PART 1: HISTORICAL FOUNDATION

## The Core Argument: "Where Tennessee Began"

### What We Can Definitively Claim

**Rocky Mount = First Capital of Southwest Territory**

- Established: 1790-1792
- Function: Territorial capital where Governor William Blount administered government
- Significance: First federal government presence south of the Ohio River
- Outcome: Led directly to Tennessee statehood in 1796
- Status: Tennessee became the 16th state, first admitted after the original 13

### The Distinction That Matters

| County         | Legitimate Claim                                  | Type of "First"         |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| **Washington** | Oldest county (1777) & oldest town (1779)         | **Chronological** first |
| **Sullivan**   | Where Tennessee's government operated (1790-1792) | **Governmental** first  |

**Both are accurate but describe different things.**

**Marketing framing:**

- Washington County: "Tennessee's Oldest County"
- Sullivan County: "Where Tennessee Began as a Government"

### Why This Distinction Works

1. **Non-competitive:** Washington keeps "oldest," Sullivan claims "where government began"
2. **Historically accurate:** Both claims are documentable
3. **Compelling narrative:** Government formation is more dynamic than chronological age
4. **America 250 alignment:** Founding story fits Semiquincentennial themes

# PART 3: COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

## Direct Competitors Analysis

### Washington County/Jonesborough

**Their Strengths:**

- Oldest county in Tennessee (1777) - documentable
- Jonesborough = oldest town (1779) - documentable
- Strong existing heritage tourism infrastructure
- "Tennessee's Oldest Town" brand is established
- Active Jonesborough History Museum

**Their Limitations:**

- "Oldest" is a static claim (doesn't tell a story)
- No territorial capital connection
- No presidential visits documented

**Our Counter-Positioning:**

- We don't compete on "oldest" - we concede that
- We own "where government began" - different narrative
- We have presidential connections (Deery Inn)
- We have the territorial capital story (Rocky Mount)

### Other East Tennessee Heritage Tourism Sites

**Sycamore Shoals (Elizabethton):**

- Strong Revolutionary War story (Overmountain Men departure point)
- Fort Watauga recreation
- Partnership opportunity, not competitor

**Exchange Place (Kingsport):**

- 1850s farm and cultural center
- Later historical period
- Partnership opportunity

**Strategy:** Position Sullivan County as the regional anchor for "Where Tennessee Began," with Sycamore Shoals, Exchange Place, and Rocky Mount as complementary sites telling Tennessee's founding story chronologically.

# PART 4: AMERICA 250 TOURISM NARRATIVE

## The Sullivan County Story Arc

### Act I: Revolutionary Foundations (1770s)

**Sites:** Rocky Mount settlement, Overmountain Men connections
**Story:** Frontier settlers carve civilization from wilderness during Revolutionary War
**Visitor Experience:** See the landscape where Tennessee's first families established farms

### Act II: Government Birth (1790-1792)

**Sites:** Rocky Mount (territorial capital)
**Story:** Governor William Blount transforms frontier into government
**Visitor Experience:** Walk where the first Southwest Territory government operated

### Act III: Presidential Era (Early 1800s)

**Sites:** Old Deery Inn
**Story:** Young nation's leaders travel through Sullivan County
**Visitor Experience:** Stand where three presidents stayed

### Act IV: Statehood Achievement (1796)

**Sites:** Sullivan County as governmental foundation
**Story:** Tennessee becomes 16th state, first after original 13
**Visitor Experience:** Understand how territorial period led to statehood

## Visitor Journey Design

**1-Day Sullivan County Heritage Tour:**

- Morning: Rocky Mount (2-3 hours) - territorial capital experience
- Lunch: Historic Blountville (1 hour)
- Afternoon: Old Deery Inn (1 hour) - three presidents story
- Optional: Historic courthouse, downtown architecture

**2-Day Regional Heritage Tour:**

- Day 1: Sullivan County (above itinerary)
- Day 2: Sycamore Shoals (Overmountain Men) + Jonesborough (oldest town)

# PART 5: RECOMMENDED MARKETING LANGUAGE

## Approved Headlines & Taglines

✅ **"Sullivan County: Where Tennessee Began"**

- Clean, simple, defensible

✅ **"Where Frontier Became Government"**

- Tells the transformation story

✅ **"First Capital of the Southwest Territory"**

- Historically specific and accurate

✅ **"Where Tennessee's Story Started"**

- Softer version of "began"

✅ **"Three Presidents, One County"**

- Deery Inn angle

## Rejected/Problematic Claims

❌ **"America's First Western Capital"**

- False - Marietta, Ohio preceded it

❌ **"First Territorial Capital"**

- False without "Southwest" qualifier

❌ **"Where George Washington Appointed Officials"**

- Misleading - he did this in Philadelphia

❌ **"Tennessee's Oldest Community"**

- False - that's Jonesborough/Washington County

## Website & Brochure Copy (Template)

### Hero Section:

> **Sullivan County, Tennessee**  
> Where Tennessee Began
>
> In 1790, Governor William Blount chose Sullivan County as the capital of the Southwest Territory. From Rocky Mount, he established the first federal government south of the Appalachian Mountains. Six years later, that territory became Tennessee—the first state admitted to the Union after the original thirteen. This is where frontier became government. This is where Tennessee began.

### Supporting Content:

> **Three Presidents Under One Roof**  
> Andrew Jackson, James K. Polk, and Andrew Johnson all stayed at the Old Deery Inn in Blountville. Few places in America can claim such presidential density.
>
> **Tennessee's Oldest Documented Farm**  
> Rocky Mount, established in 1775, has been in continuous agricultural use for over 250 years. Walk the same ground where Governor Blount governed.
>
> **Revolutionary War Heritage**  
> Sullivan County's families supplied the Overmountain Men who marched to Kings Mountain in 1780, securing a crucial Revolutionary War victory.

# PART 11: RISK MITIGATION

## Identified Risks & Responses

### Risk 1: Washington County Disputes "Where Tennessee Began" Claim

**Likelihood:** Medium  
**Impact:** Medium  
**Mitigation:**

- Emphasize distinction between "oldest" and "where government began"
- Offer partnership rather than competition
- Use precise historical language that's defensible

### Risk 2: Old Deery Inn Restoration Costs Exceed Capacity

**Likelihood:** High  
**Impact:** High  
**Mitigation:**

- Commission professional assessment early
- Develop phased restoration plan
- Pursue federal preservation tax credits
- Consider public-private partnership

### Risk 3: Limited Tourism Infrastructure in Sullivan County

**Likelihood:** Medium  
**Impact:** Medium  
**Mitigation:**

- Partner with Kingsport/Bristol for lodging
- Develop half-day itineraries (less lodging-dependent)
- Target drive-market visitors (within 2 hours)

### Risk 4: Rocky Mount Capacity Limitations

**Likelihood:** Low  
**Impact:** Medium  
**Mitigation:**

- Stagger programming throughout 2026
- Use outdoor spaces for overflow
- Partner with nearby sites for additional capacity
