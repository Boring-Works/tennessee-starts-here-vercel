## Mission Summary

Verify the claim: "Andrew Jackson lodged at Rocky Mount for six weeks in Spring 1788"

By examining:

- Jackson's documented 1788 timeline
- Primary source evidence availability
- Building dendrochronology (dates)
- Geographic route analysis
- Competing location claims
- Cross-references with other historical documents

---

## Finding

**THE CLAIM IS UNVERIFIED**

No primary source documentation exists to support the Rocky Mount claim. All evidence comes from secondary sources (books/museums written 100+ years later). The buildings visitors see were constructed 38-42 years AFTER Jackson's claimed visit.

---

✓ **September 1787:** Jackson admitted to North Carolina bar

- Source: NC bar admission records
- Location: Salisbury
- Confidence: 100%

✓ **August 1788:** Jackson in Jonesborough for duel challenge incident

- Source: Multiple biographical sources, specific individuals named
- Location: Jonesborough, TN
- Confidence: 100%

✓ **October 1788:** Jackson arrives in Nashville as district attorney

- Source: Appointment records
- Location: Nashville, TN
- Confidence: 100%

## What We DON'T KNOW (The Rocky Mount Gap)

✗ **Spring 1788:** Claimed six-week stay at Rocky Mount

- Primary sources: ZERO
- Secondary sources: Yes, but all repeat same claim without verification
- Documentary evidence: NONE
- Confidence: ~30% (tradition only)

---

## Three Critical Problems

### Problem 1: NO PRIMARY SOURCES (CRITICAL)

**The Evidence:**

- NO Jackson letters mentioning Rocky Mount
- NO Cobb family receipts or records
- NO contemporary 1788 accounts
- NO diary entries from Jackson or anyone else

**What This Means:**

- Claim cannot be independently verified
- All evidence is hearsay (secondary sources)
- 100+ year old tradition with no foundation

**Severity:** CRITICAL — Zero primary documentation exists

### Problem 2: PHYSICAL IMPOSSIBILITY (CRITICAL)

**The Evidence:**

- University of Tennessee dendrochronology study (Grissino-Mayer & van de Gevel, 2007)
- Current Rocky Mount structures built: **1826-1830**
- Jackson's claimed visit: **1788**
- Time gap: **38-42 YEARS**

**What This Means:**

- Jackson could NOT have stayed in buildings visitors see today
- Buildings were built by Michael Massengill (Cobb's grandson)
- If Jackson visited 1788, it was in different structure (no evidence remains)
- Visitors currently believe they're seeing where Jackson stayed (FALSE)

**Severity:** CRITICAL — Visiting experience is misleading

### Problem 3: GEOGRAPHIC INEFFICIENCY (HIGH)

**The Evidence:**

- Jackson needed Jonesborough (where his legal business was)
- Rocky Mount is 30 miles PAST Jonesborough
- Direct route via Wilderness Road is more efficient
- 130+ mile detour adds 8-12 extra travel days

**What This Means:**

- Route makes no logical sense
- Why would he detour to Rocky Mount when his business was in Jonesborough?
- No documented reason for this inefficient routing

**Severity:** HIGH — Doesn't align with rational travel patterns
