## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Tennessee Starts Here content repository is a **sophisticated research operation that has produced far more knowledge than appears on the public website**. We have:

- **15 major research reports** on overlooked populations (enslaved people, free Black people, women, working-class settlers, religious minorities)
- **10 evidence review reports** diagnosing representation gaps and scholarly standards
- **12 advisory documents** with strategic recommendations
- **6 collection definitions** (all with content)
- **41 primary source documents** (Blount papers, treaties, Cherokee documents)
- **50 biographical profiles** (detailed, well-researched)
- Only **~30% of research findings are currently visible on the website**

**The gold that got buried:** Hundreds of hours of research into marginalized voices, diversity gaps, and scholarly standards improvements exist in the /content folder but are not yet reflected in the live Evidence Room.

### HIGH-PRIORITY RESEARCH NOT ON WEBSITE

1. **Free Black Communities (361 people in 1790 territory)**
   - Research: Complete pathway identified (Margaret Lee case, tax records, militia rolls)
   - Documents: One freedom case available (Margaret Lee, 1795, Washington District)
   - Status: Research done; digitization not yet funded
   - Impact: Demonstrates free Black legal participation and property ownership

2. **Enslaved People at Rocky Mount**
   - Research: Architectural evidence documented (kitchen building with "quarters for domestic servants," 1791 inventory)
   - Documents: Inventory available but not highlighted
   - Status: Research complete; sensitive topic requires interpretive framework
   - Impact: Reveals labor behind Rocky Mount's $2,000 valuation and glass windows

   - Research: Barsheba Cobb documented managing household/kitchen feeding 42 Cherokee chiefs (July 1791)

4. **Working-Class Settler Petitions**
   - Research: Identified in archives (e.g., August 1794 petition against 25-cent tax; militia petitions)

5. **Ordinary Militia Members**
   - Research: Colonel Henley's "Wastebook" (1793-1798) contains complete militia rolls

7. **Mary Patton Primary Source Verification**

8. **Religious Minorities**
   - Research: Noted as gap in EVIDENCE-REVIEW-DIVERSITY-INCLUSION.md
   - Documents: Not yet researched
   - Status: Research pathway identified; work not yet started
   - Impact: Would represent Quakers, Methodists, Jews, and other minority faith communities

9. **Scots-Irish Cultural Heritage**
   - Research: Listed as research report but content not examined

## CONCLUSION

The Tennessee Starts Here content operation has produced **excellent research-grade analysis of Tennessee's founding**. The knowledge base is well-organized, internally consistent, and clearly documents both what is known and what remains to be discovered.

However, **the strategic analysis layer (EVIDENCE-REVIEW and ADVISORY documents) is far more sophisticated than what appears on the public website**. Visitors see a solid Evidence Room; leadership sees detailed assessments of its gaps.

**The biggest opportunities for impact:**

1. Implement free Black people documentation (ready to go; 2-3 hours)
2. Contextualize enslaved people evidence (research done; needs presentation framework; 2-3 hours)
3. Highlight working-class voices (archival pathway clear; 5-8 hours)
4. Expand stub profiles (lowest-hanging fruit; 5-10 hours)
5. Create educator materials (identified; not yet built; 4-6 hours)

**Total estimated work to significantly improve diversity representation and visitor experience: 25-35 hours.**

This knowledge is valuable, well-researched, and strategically important. It deserves implementation.
