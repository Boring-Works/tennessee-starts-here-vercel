# Archive Discovery Workflow

**Purpose:** Systematic process for discovering and acquiring new historical documents

**Created:** January 30, 2026
**Status:** Active operational workflow
**Scope:** All document acquisition for Rocky Mount State Historic Site Evidence Room

---

## Overview

This workflow establishes a **systematic, repeatable process** for:

1. **Strategic discovery** - Quarterly searches of major archives
2. **Acquisition** - Obtaining documents through various pathways
3. **Intake & registration** - Processing new materials into verification queue

**Goal:** Never rely on chance discoveries. Proactively search for historical materials on a predictable schedule.

---

## PHASE 1: Strategic Discovery

### Quarterly Archive Review Schedule

Rocky Mount conducts **systematic searches** of major archives on a quarterly rotation:

#### Q1 (January-March): Federal Archives Focus

**Archives to search:**

- Library of Congress (Manuscript Division, Rare Book Room)
- National Archives (RG 75 - Bureau of Indian Affairs, RG 11 - General Records)
- Founders Online (Washington, Jefferson, Adams papers)
- American State Papers (Indian Affairs, Public Lands)

**Why Q1:** Federal fiscal year planning, slower visitor season allows research time

#### Q2 (April-June): State & Regional Archives

**Archives to search:**

- Tennessee State Library & Archives (Territorial Papers, County Records)
- North Carolina State Archives (Colonial/State Records 1663-1820)
- Virginia Historical Society (Southwest Virginia collections)
- East Tennessee Historical Society (Local manuscripts)

**Why Q2:** State archives often accessible by appointment, spring research trips feasible

#### Q3 (July-September): University Collections

**Archives to search:**

- University of Tennessee Special Collections (Knoxville - Ramsey Papers, local history)
- Vanderbilt University (James Robertson Papers, Tennessee history)
- UNC Chapel Hill Southern Historical Collection (Frontier/backcountry materials)
- Virginia Tech Special Collections (Southwest Virginia history)

**Why Q3:** Summer = student workers available to assist, professors accessible for consultation

#### Q4 (October-December): Specialized & Private Collections

**Archives to search:**

- Cherokee Nation Archives (Tahlequah, OK - partnership request)
- Local historical societies (Sullivan County, Washington County)
- Family collections (Cobb descendants, Massengill family)
- Private manuscript dealers (monitor catalogs for relevant offerings)

**Why Q4:** Year-end wrap-up, budget available for purchases if needed, holiday downtime for research

### Systematic Search Strategy

For each archive visit/search, use this **standardized search term matrix**:

#### TIER 1: Core Terms (Always Search)

1. **"Rocky Mount"**
   - Exact phrase search
   - Variant spellings: "Rockymount", "Rock Mount", "Rocky-Mount"

2. **"William Cobb" + "Tennessee"**
   - Property owner 1770s-1796
   - Also search: "Cobb's" (possessive, as in "Cobb's plantation")

3. **"Southwest Territory"**
   - Official name 1790-1796
   - Variant: "South Western Territory"

4. **"William Blount"**
   - Governor 1790-1796
   - Variant spellings: "Wm Blount", "Gov. Blount"

5. **"Treaty of Holston"**
   - July 2, 1791 - major event at Rocky Mount
   - Also: "Holston Treaty", "Treaty with Cherokee 1791"

#### TIER 2: Secondary Terms (Contextual Documents)

6. **"Washington County" + date range "1770-1800"**
   - Rocky Mount located in Washington County (then later Sullivan)
   - Captures local government records

7. **"Sullivan County" + date range "1779-1800"**
   - County created 1779 from Washington County
   - Rocky Mount on border, relevant to both

8. **"Holston" + "frontier"**
   - Holston River region = frontier settlements
   - Captures broader geographic context

9. **Names of all 42 Cherokee signatories**
   - See list in `/content/people/`
   - Examples: Standing Turkey, Hanging Maw, Bloody Fellow, etc.

10. **"Knoxville" + "territorial"**
    - Capital moved from Rocky Mount to Knoxville 1791
    - Documents about Knoxville may reference Rocky Mount

#### TIER 3: Tertiary Terms (Broader Context)

11. **"Great Road" + "Virginia"**
    - Major travel route passing Rocky Mount
    - Trade/migration context

12. **"Wilderness Road"**
    - Alternate frontier route
    - Settlement patterns

13. **"Overmountain" + settlement**
    - Settlers crossing Appalachians
    - Cultural/demographic context

14. **"Wataugans" OR "Watauga Association"**
    - Early self-government 1772
    - Pre-territorial political context

15. **Specific frontier families** from existing documents
    - Names appearing in Rocky Mount documents
    - Family networks, business connections

## PHASE 2: Acquisition Process

### Acquisition Decision Matrix

Not every discovered document should be acquired. Use this matrix:

| Priority   | Criteria                                                                                                                                              | Examples                                                                                                                 | Action                                                    |
| ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **HIGH**   | - Fills major gap in collection<br>- Cherokee voices<br>- Women's perspectives<br>- Contradicts existing narrative<br>- Directly mentions Rocky Mount | - Bloody Fellow letters<br>- Barsheba Cobb documents<br>- Land deed for property<br>- Document challenging Jackson claim | Acquire immediately                                       |
| **MEDIUM** | - Adds context to known events<br>- New perspective on known people<br>- Local governance records<br>- Frontier daily life details                    | - County court records<br>- Merchant ledgers<br>- Letters between Blount associates<br>- Territorial legislation         | Acquire if resources permit                               |
| **LOW**    | - Duplicates existing material<br>- Tangentially related<br>- Poor condition/illegible<br>- Already well-documented                                   | - Third copy of Treaty of Holston<br>- Blount letter about unrelated topic<br>- Damaged document with existing good copy | Note existence, do not acquire unless exceptional quality |

### Acquisition Pathways

#### Pathway 1: Free/Public Domain Sources (FASTEST)

**Sources:**

- Founders Online (founders.archives.gov)
- Library of Congress digital collections
- National Archives Catalog
- Google Books (pre-1928 materials)
- HathiTrust Digital Library

**Process:**

1. Download directly from website (PDF or TIFF)
2. Save to `/Historical/raw/transcripts/[source-name]/[document-id].pdf`
3. Document in `/Historical/raw/sources.json`:
   ```json
   {
     "id": "washington-to-blount-1790-06-08",
     "files": {
       "scan": "transcripts/founders-online/washington-to-blount-1790-06-08.pdf"
     },
     "provenance": {
       "original_archive": "Library of Congress",
       "original_identifier": "Washington Papers, Series 4, Image 1234",
       "obtained_date": "2026-02-15",
       "obtained_by": "Dr. Eleanor Hartwell",
       "acquisition_method": "Downloaded from Founders Online",
       "cost": "$0.00"
     }
   }
   ```
4. Add to verification queue

**Time required:** 30 minutes per document

#### Pathway 2: Digitization Requests (MODERATE SPEED)

**When to use:** Archive has document but not digitized, OR existing scan is poor quality

**Process:**

1. Contact archive with specific identifier

   ```
   Subject: Digitization Request - [Document Title]

   Dear [Archive Name] Staff,

   I am requesting a high-resolution scan of the following document:

   - Catalog ID: [Number]
   - Document: [Title/Description]
   - Date: [If known]
   - Collection: [Collection name]

   Preferred format: TIFF (600dpi) or high-quality PDF

   This is for historical preservation and educational use at Rocky Mount
   State Historic Site. Please advise on any fees or restrictions.

   Thank you,
   [Your name]
   Rocky Mount State Historic Site
   ```

2. Pay fees if required
   - **Budget guideline:** $25-50 per document typical
   - Get approval from Director if cost exceeds $50

3. Timeline: 2-6 weeks (varies by archive)

4. Save to `/Historical/raw/scans/[archive-name]/`

5. Document acquisition date and cost in `sources.json`

**Time required:** 2-6 weeks from request to receipt

#### Pathway 3: Physical Archive Visits (THOROUGH)

**When to use:** Multiple documents in one location, OR document requires in-person examination

**Pre-visit preparation:**

1. Schedule research appointment (2-4 weeks advance notice typical)
2. Review archive policies (photography allowed? Fees? Retrieval limits?)
3. Create checklist of documents to examine
4. Bring: camera, laptop, notepad, archive call slips, pencils only

**Onsite process:**

1. Request documents from archive staff (usually 1-3 at a time)
2. Examine for relevance
3. Photograph OR take detailed notes on provenance
4. If photography not allowed: Order copies from archive
5. Complete call slips/order forms before leaving

**Post-visit:**

1. Organize photos/scans into `/Historical/raw/scans/[archive-name]/`
2. Update discovery log with findings
3. If copies ordered: Wait 2-4 weeks for delivery

**Time required:** 1 day onsite + 2-4 weeks for ordered copies

**Budget:** $100-300 (travel, fees, copies)

#### Pathway 4: Purchase from Dealers (RARE)

**When to use:** Historical document appears for sale from reputable dealer

**Process:**

1. **Verify authenticity** before purchase (consult expert if needed)
2. **Check provenance** - how did dealer acquire it?
3. **Director approval required** for ANY purchase over $100
4. Request condition report and high-res images
5. Negotiate price if possible
6. Document full provenance chain in `sources.json`

**Budget:** Varies widely ($100-$5,000+ for significant items)

**Caution:** Forgeries exist. Always verify before buying.

### Staff Contributions & Visitor Discoveries

#### Staff Discovery Protocol

**If staff member finds a historical document:**

1. **Do NOT touch/move** original if fragile
2. **Photograph** in situ (where found) if possible
3. **Fill out discovery form:**

```
HISTORICAL DOCUMENT DISCOVERY FORM

Your Name: _______________
Date Found: _______________
Where found: _______________

Document details:
□ Letter   □ Newspaper   □ Photo   □ Book   □ Map   □ Other: _______

Date on document (if visible): _______________
Who created it (if known): _______________
What it's about: _______________

Why it might be important: _______________

□ Photo attached
□ Brought to Director's office

Staff signature: _______________
```

4. **Deliver to Director within 24 hours**

#### Visitor Contribution Protocol

**If visitor offers to share historical document/photo/information:**

1. **Thank them enthusiastically**
   - "This is wonderful! We're building a comprehensive historical archive."

2. **Photograph or scan document WITH PERMISSION**
   - Use phone/camera to capture image
   - If fragile: Take photo, do not handle excessively

3. **Record visitor information:**

   ```
   Visitor Contribution Form

   Date: _______________
   Visitor name: _______________
   Contact email/phone: _______________

   Document description: _______________
   How they acquired it: _______________

   □ Permission granted to include in archive
   □ Visitor requests credit/acknowledgment
   □ Visitor wishes to remain anonymous

   Staff member: _______________
   ```

4. **Send to Director for evaluation within 24 hours**

5. **If valuable:**
   - Request written permission to include
   - Acknowledge contributor in document metadata
   - Send thank-you note/email

6. **If not relevant:**
   - Still thank visitor
   - Explain (kindly) why it doesn't fit collection scope
   - Suggest alternative archives if appropriate

## PHASE 3: Intake & Registration

### Step 1: Initial Assessment (Director Only)

When new document arrives (via any pathway), Director reviews:

#### Assessment Checklist:

- [ ] **Relevance:** Does this relate to Rocky Mount, SW Territory, or frontier history 1770-1800?
- [ ] **Authenticity:** Does it appear genuine? (If questionable, consult expert)
- [ ] **Duplicates:** Do we already have this document?
- [ ] **Condition:** Is it legible/usable?
- [ ] **Priority:** HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW (per acquisition matrix)

#### Decision:

**Option A: Add to Collection**

- Proceed to Step 2 (Register in sources.json)
- High/Medium priority → Immediate verification queue
- Low priority → Future verification queue

**Option B: Note and File**

- Document exists but not high enough priority
- Record in discovery log: "Found but not acquired"
- May revisit later if priorities change

**Option C: Reject**

- Not relevant OR authenticity questionable
- Document decision in discovery log with reasoning

### Step 2: Register in sources.json

For documents added to collection, create entry in `/Historical/raw/sources.json`:

```json
{
  "id": "unique-document-identifier-YYYY-MM-DD",
  "files": {
    "scan": "scans/archive-name/filename.pdf",
    "transcript": "transcripts/archive-name/filename.txt"
  },
  "metadata": {
    "title": "Letter from William Blount to Henry Knox",
    "date": "1790-10-20",
    "author": "William Blount",
    "recipient": "Henry Knox",
    "document_type": "correspondence"
  },
  "provenance": {
    "original_archive": "Library of Congress",
    "original_identifier": "Washington Papers, Series 4, Image 1234",
    "collection": "George Washington Papers",
    "obtained_date": "2026-02-15",
    "obtained_by": "Dr. Eleanor Hartwell",
    "acquisition_method": "Downloaded from Founders Online",
    "cost": "$0.00",
    "copyright_status": "Public domain"
  },
  "discovery_notes": "Found during Q1 2026 systematic search of LOC. Confirms Rocky Mount selected as territorial capital before Blount's arrival."
}
```

**ID naming convention:** `[topic]-[date]-[optional-detail]`

- Examples: `blount-arrival-1790-10-20`, `treaty-holston-1791-07-02`, `cobb-deed-1775`

## Annual Discovery Goals

### Minimum Targets (Sustainable):

- **Q1:** 5-10 documents discovered, 3-5 acquired
- **Q2:** 5-10 documents discovered, 3-5 acquired
- **Q3:** 3-8 documents discovered, 2-4 acquired (summer research trips)
- **Q4:** 2-5 documents discovered, 1-3 acquired (lower priority season)

**Annual total:** 15-30 new documents added to collection

### Stretch Goals (With Grant Funding):

- 40-60 documents per year
- Hire research assistant for systematic searches
- Travel to distant archives (Cherokee Nation, NC State Archives)

## Special Focus: Cherokee Documents

Cherokee perspectives are underrepresented in most archives (U.S. perspective dominates). **Special efforts required:**

### Strategies:

1. **Partnership with Cherokee Nation Archives**
   - Formal request for collaboration
   - Offer to share digitized materials
   - Credit Cherokee Nation prominently

2. **Search for Cherokee speeches/statements**
   - Recorded in U.S. government documents
   - Newspaper reports of Cherokee visits
   - Treaty negotiation minutes

3. **Consult Cherokee historians**
   - Academic experts on Cherokee history
   - Tribal cultural preservation officers
   - Request guidance on interpretation

4. **Acknowledge limitations**
   - U.S. records filtered through translator/scribe
   - Original Cherokee words often lost
   - Written word may not capture full meaning

**Goal:** By 2027, have Cherokee perspectives on every major event in collection.
