# Event Passport Program Specification

## Rocky Mount State Historic Site - 2026 America 250 Season

**Purpose:** Create three tiered passport programs that reward multi-event attendance, increase repeat visits, and deepen visitor engagement with the narrative arc of the season.

**Strategy:** Passports convert episodic attendance into seasonal commitment. They create a "collect them all" mentality that drives calendar planning and word-of-mouth marketing.

---

## PROGRAM OVERVIEW

### Three Passport Tiers

| Tier       | Name               | Target Visitor                 | Season Goal | Completion Reward           |
| ---------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------ | ----------- | --------------------------- |
| **Tier 1** | Frontier Explorer  | Families, casual visitors      | 5-8 events  | $25 gift card + discount    |
| **Tier 2** | Government Witness | Educators, history enthusiasts | 8-12 events | $50 gear + exclusive access |
| **Tier 3** | First Family       | Genealogy, deep engagement     | 12+ events  | VIP status + naming rights  |

---

## TIER 1: FRONTIER EXPLORER PASSPORT

**Tagline:** "Visit Five Frontiers. Claim Your Story."

### Eligibility & Participation

- **Who:** Families with kids (primary) + casual individual visitors
- **Starting Point:** Road to 250 (March 4) — receive passport booklet
- **Entry Mechanic:** Free with registration at any event

### Checkpoint Events (5 Required, Choose 4 of These 7)

```
SPRING CHECK (Choose 1)
- Road to 250 (Mar 4)
- Spring Break Camp (Mar 16-20 or Mar 24-27)
- Woolly Days (Apr 25-26)

EARLY SUMMER CHECK (Choose 1)
- Stitching Independence (Jun 13-14)
- Summer Camp (Jun 15-19 or Jun 22-26)

PEAK SUMMER CHECK (Required)
- Colonial Independence Day (Jul 4) ★

FALL CHECK (Choose 2)
- Harvest Fest (Oct 10-11)
- Haunting on the Mount (Oct 15-24)
- Cherokee Heritage Weekend (Aug 22-23)

WINTER CHECK (Choose 1)
- Frontier Christmas (Nov 4-21)
- Candlelight Christmas (Dec 4-12)
```

### Passport Design

**Physical Format:** 32-page booklet (5.5" x 8.5"), color

- Cover: Frontier compass rose, "Explore. Discover. Remember. 1791-2026"
- Each event gets a full-page spread with:
  - Event name, date, location
  - Blank space for QR code scan OR staff stamp
  - Space to write: "What I learned" (1-2 lines)
  - Illustration relevant to event theme
- Perforated "achievement stickers" in back (removable)

**QR Code System:**

- Each event has a unique QR code
- Attendees scan with smartphone to "check in"
- Phone shows: "Great! 1 of 5 stops toward Frontier Explorer status"
- Offline backup: Staff stamp with event date

### Completion Incentive

**Upon Completing 5 Events:**

- Bring passport to any gate (or mail in)
- Receive:
  - **$25 gift card** to Rocky Mount gift shop OR
  - **Free admission** to any 2026 event (before Dec 31)
  - **Digital certificate** (printable/social media shareable)
- Staff photo opportunity: "I'm a Frontier Explorer" holding passport

**Promotion Messaging:**

- "Collect 5 stamps. Unlock the story. Claim your discount."
- Passports available at check-in at Road to 250 and all subsequent events
- Digital version: QR code to download/print passport

### Business Model

- Print cost: $1.20 per passport × 500 = $600
- Gift cards cost: $25 × estimated 150 completions = $3,750
- **ROI:** High (families spending $400-500 over season earn $25 back)
- **Analytics:** Track which events are most popular checkpoints

## TIER 2: GOVERNMENT WITNESS PASSPORT

**Tagline:** "Eight Events. The Full Story. Witness Tennessee's Birth."

### Eligibility & Participation

- **Who:** Educators, history enthusiasts, adult learners, genealogy-curious visitors
- **Starting Point:** Lecture series (any lecture) OR Early Frontier Days
- **Entry Mechanic:** Free; must register online or at gate

### Checkpoint Events (8 Required, Choose 6 of These 9)

```
LECTURE SERIES (Choose all 5)
- Byrd: Colonial Religion (Mar 27)
- Patton: Powder for Kings Mountain (Apr 18)
- Bachelor: The Overmountain Men's Gamble (May 15)
- Whitfield: William Blount and Tennessee Government (May 29)
- Doan: The Story of the American Flag (Jun 13)

FOUNDATION EVENTS (Choose 2 of 3)
- Sullivan County 250 (Apr 17)
- Early Frontier Days (May 22-24)
- Stitching Independence (Jun 13-14)

PERSPECTIVE EVENTS (Choose 1 of 2)
- Cherokee Heritage Weekend (Aug 22-23)
- First Families Reunion (Sep 11-13)
```

### Passport Design

**Physical Format:** 20-page booklet (6" x 9"), premium card stock, full color

- Cover: Parchment texture, "A Government Witness to Tennessee's Birth - 1791-2026"
- Branded section headers:
  - "The Road to Kings Mountain" (religion/context section)
  - "The March & The Gamble" (Revolutionary section)
  - "The Capitol Arrives" (government section)
  - "The Nation Remembers" (contemporary section)
- Each lecture gets 2 pages:
  - Left: Speaker bio, talk description, topics
  - Right: Blank note-taking space + space to write "Key Insight"
- Festival pages include QR code for digital note-taking backup
- Back 3 pages: "My Tennessee Story" (journaling prompts)

**Digital Component:**

- QR code on cover links to online passport tracker
- Login with email, track progress in real-time
- Export your notes as PDF (shareable, reviewable)

### Completion Incentive

**Upon Completing 8 Events:**

- Digital notification: "Congratulations, Government Witness!"
- Receive:
  - **$50 gear item** (Rocky Mount branded: journal, mug, or heritage seed packet)
  - **VIP access to 2026 Behind the Scenes experiences** (2 free visits, no waiting list)
  - **Exclusive digital asset**: "Government Witness Certificate" (signed by Executive Director)
  - **Annual subscription discount**: 25% off all 2027 events
- Invitation to "Government Witness Reunion" (Feb 2027)
- Name listed on website under "Witnesses" (with permission)

**Promotion Messaging:**

- "This is not a casual visit. This is an education. Eight events. Eight chapters. One story."
- Position as "curated learning journey" — not just collecting stamps
- "Share your notes. Share your insights. Become part of the archive."

### Business Model

- Print cost: $2.80 per passport × 200 = $560
- Gear items: $50 × estimated 50 completions = $2,500
- Behind the Scenes capacity cost: Minimal (already running monthly)
- **ROI:** Moderate (educators become annual advocates + multi-event spenders)
- **Analytics:** Track which lectures are most engaging (note-taking depth)

## TIER 3: FIRST FAMILY PASSPORT

**Tagline:** "Become Part of Rocky Mount's Permanent Story."

### Eligibility & Participation

- **Who:** Genealogy researchers, descendants, deep-history enthusiasts, multi-year repeat visitors
- **Starting Point:** First Families Reunion (Sep 11-13) OR OVTA Victory Day (Sep 24)
- **Entry Mechanic:** By invitation OR application (must show intent to complete all events)

### Checkpoint Events (10+ Required, ALL of These)

```
MANDATORY EVENTS (ALL 10)
1. Early Frontier Days (May 22-24)
2. Stitching Independence (Jun 13-14)
3. Colonial Independence Day (Jul 4) ★ [MUST ATTEND]
4. Cherokee Heritage Weekend (Aug 22-23)
5. First Families Reunion (Sep 11-13) ★ [MUST ATTEND]
6. OVTA Victory Day (Sep 24)
7. Harvest Fest (Oct 10-11)
8. Haunting on the Mount (Oct 15-24)
9. Frontier Christmas (Nov 4-21)
10. Candlelight Christmas (Dec 4-12)

BONUS COMPLETIONS (3 of these)
- All 5 Lectures (earn "Scholar" badge)
- 2+ Summer Camps (with family)
- 2+ Behind the Scenes experiences
- 4+ Blacksmith Workshops
- Twilight Tours (4+, earn "Evening Explorer" badge)
```

### Passport Design

**Physical Format:** Premium leather-bound journal (8" x 10"), embossed, archival-quality paper

- Cover: Embossed Rocky Mount seal + "First Family Passport - 1791-2026"
- Personalization: "This book belongs to [name]" (handwritten by staff)
- Numbered edition: "First Family #[sequential number]" (e.g., "First Family #47")
- Ribbon bookmark with dangling brass tag

**Content:**

- Introductory letter from Executive Director
- Family tree template (if visiting with descendants)
- 40 pages of premium stock for notes, drawings, photos
- Each event has a dedicated section with:
  - Event date, place, attendees
  - Illustrations or historical excerpts
  - Prompts: "Where was I? Who was with me? What changed for me?"
  - Space for pressed flowers, ticket stubs, sketches
- Back section: "The Story I'll Tell"
- Closing: Signature line for Executive Director + staff attendants

**Digital Companion:**

- QR code on inside cover links to private online archive
- Upload photos from each event (private gallery)
- View your "First Family story" as an interactive timeline
- Download annual "your story" PDF

### Completion Incentive

**Upon Completing All 10 Events:**

**Immediate:**

- **Naming Rights**: Your name added to permanent "First Families of 2026" plaque in visitor center (with permission)
- **Gold Medal**: Actual engraved medal (2" diameter, on lanyard)
- **VIP Experience**: Private tour of collections vault + 1-hour curator consultation
- **Annual Recognition**: Invited as "Honored Guest" to opening day 2027 + featured in newsletter

**Long-Term:**

- **Lifetime Pass**: Free admission to Rocky Mount for 5 years (transferable to 1 family member)
- **Archive Contribution**: Offer to donate your passport pages to museum archives (curated collection of "visitor journeys")
- **Committee Opportunity**: Invitation to serve on Rocky Mount's Visitor Advisory Board (quarterly meetings)
- **Legacy Status**: "First Family" designation on your membership card (if applicable)

**Social Proof:**

- "You've visited every major event of the America 250 season. You ARE the story."
- Featured in social media: "Meet [Name], a First Family of 2026" (with photo + 50-word bio)
- Newsletter spotlight (quarterly)

**Promotion Messaging:**

- "This is not a passport. It's a commitment. It's a legacy."
- Position as highest honor available to visitors
- "Your children will ask you, 'Why were you there?' This book is your answer."

### Business Model

- Passport journals: $8 per book × 50 = $400
- Engraved medals: $12 × 50 = $600
- VIP curator time: Estimated 40 hours × $0 (staff time)
- Lifetime passes: Cost-benefit (5-year forgone revenue ~$750 × 50 = $37,500) BUT retention value is high
- **ROI:** Very high long-term (creates institutional memory, advocacy, and annual donors)
- **Analytics:** Track which events drive deepest engagement; identify future board candidates

## TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

### QR Code System

**Structure:**

- 1 unique QR code per event
- QR code links to: `rockymountmuseum.com/passport?event=[event-id]&visitor=[email]`
- No backend database needed initially (use Google Forms as backend)

**Data Captured:**

```
Email, Event ID, Timestamp, Passport Tier, IP Address
```

**Offline Backup:**

- Staff stamps with event name + date
- Visitors can email stamp photos for digital verification

**MVP Implementation:**

- Month 1: Print QR codes, train staff, set up Google Form
- Month 2: Launch digital tracker (basic web form → Google Sheets)
- Month 3+: Analyze data, refine messaging

### Fulfillment

**Tier 1 (Frontier Explorer):**

- Voucher printed at event or emailed
- Redeemable at gift shop OR applied to next ticket purchase

**Tier 2 (Government Witness):**

- Gear item ordered/printed upon completion
- Mailed within 2 weeks or picked up at museum

**Tier 3 (First Family):**

- Leather journal engraved with number (custom print order: 2-3 week lead time)
- Medal engraved and shipped
- Plaque text compiled at season end, installed by Spring 2027

## CONTINGENCY & SCALING

### If Tier 1 Completion Rate is < 30%

**Actions:**

- Simplify to 4 events (instead of 5)
- Lower reward value ($15 gift card)
- Add "bonus" checkpoints (workshops, tours) to make completion easier
- Increase staff reminder messaging

### If Tier 2 Completion Rate is < 20%

**Actions:**

- Make lectures optional; allow substitution with other events
- Lower completion requirement to 6 events
- Offer digital certificate only (no $50 gear)
- Create "lecture bundle" discount to drive attendance

### If Tier 3 Gets More Than 100 Applications

**Actions:**

- Use weighted scoring: prioritize genealogy researchers + repeat visitors
- Create waitlist for following year
- Increase medal/journal production

### Scaling to 2027+

**Year 2 Planning:**

- Offer "returning First Family" benefits (discount, early booking)
- Create "Platinum" tier for multi-year completers
- Build passport data into membership/season pass system

## RISK MITIGATION

### Risk: Low QR Code Adoption

- **Mitigation:** Always offer offline stamp backup; don't require technology
- **Backup:** Simple punch card with holes, no QR needed

### Risk: Staff Doesn't Promote Passports

- **Mitigation:** Monthly staff incentives; track who scans the most QR codes; recognition board
- **Backup:** Pre-made scripts; printed job aids at every checkpoint

### Risk: Completion Rewards Cost More Than Budgeted

- **Mitigation:** Set completion caps (max 100 Tier 3, max 50 Tier 2)
- **Backup:** Substitute digital certificates for physical rewards if needed

### Risk: Tier 3 Too Exclusive, Creates Perception of Elitism

- **Mitigation:** Celebrate ALL completers equally on social media; feature Tier 1 & 2 stories too
- **Backup:** Create "honorary" First Family status for close non-completers

## CONCLUSION

The three-tier passport program converts seasonal attendance into a layered engagement ladder:

- **Tier 1** creates family habit and baseline repeat visitation
- **Tier 2** builds educator/enthusiast community and deep narrative understanding
- **Tier 3** establishes loyalty, legacy, and institutional advocacy

Together, they transform Rocky Mount from "a place you visit" into "a place you belong."
